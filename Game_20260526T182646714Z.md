They are the kind of still that is waiting and the kind of waiting that has decided something already. Their eyes catch the beam and throw it back — amber, not blue, not red. The same amber the lens cycles through. The device recognizes them.

Damian's voice carries through the light: *They're not stopping. The light isn't hurting me — it's marking me. They're waiting for it to finish.*

The lens adjusts again. The beam narrows. The color shifts from blue-white to amber, deep and hot, the color of something that has made a decision. The subsonic hum in the room where you stand changes pitch — higher, tighter, the sound of something building toward a point. The figure that was in this room is not in this room. The figure is in the courtyard with the others. You can see it now in the light: the same tall, narrow shape, the same stillness, one of the watchers wearing what the room's occupant was wearing before the room had an occupant.

The document is still in your coat. The stamp is still in its cradle in the office behind you. The device is between you and the arch.

What do you do?
