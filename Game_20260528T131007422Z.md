The blue is not water. It is the space before water existed — before form, before you. You drift. There is no surface to reach for, no floor to sink to, no horizon to navigate by. You are formless awareness suspended in an ocean that belongs to no one.

You are not drowning. Drowning requires a body, and you have not quite arrived at one yet. There is only the drift, and the faint sense — increasingly difficult to ignore — that something in you is taking shape. A concept. A single principle you are clinging to without yet knowing you are clinging to anything at all.

Then: a voice. Not from beside you. Not from below. From somewhere outside the blue entirely — and it says your name.

Or it doesn't. Or the blue thins and something else happens. You tell me what occurs.

What do you hold onto, and what is beginning to become?
