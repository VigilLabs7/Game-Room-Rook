The blue is absolute. You are in it and it is all there is — no up, no down, no sense of where the blue ends and something else might begin. You are aware, but the awareness has no container yet, no name, no edges. There is one thing you are holding onto, the only solid thing in all this dissolving blue — you don't know what it is, only that without it you would come apart entirely. From somewhere outside the blue, from above it, a voice reaches down. It sounds like it knows you. It says words that almost make the thing you're holding feel like it could become a name. The blue thins. You begin to take shape around the truth you are gripping.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** [player-alpha] I claw upward through the blue toward the voice, toward the name it's almost giving me — and the thing I am holding is the only solid thing left. IAM NOTUM SUM. I say it and I mean it. The blue thins. I arrive at the fountain and I am already named, already gripping the truth that will not let me come apart. The inscription is carved into the stone: IAM NOTUM SUM. Someone was here before me. Someone carved it and meant it. I press my hand to the stone and ask it: what is the conduit carrying underneath you?

**Game Master:**

The words leave you and the blue lets go.

Not a release — a rearrangement. The formless that held you shucks itself off like a garment you forgot you were wearing, and you are standing, and the ground is stone, and the sound is water — real water, close, silver and continuous, chuckling against the basin rim like it has been laughing at something for a very long time.

You are at the fountain. The desert is behind you but you did not walk it. Your knees are clean. Your feet are your own. The body was earned in a life you cannot yet remember, and it holds.

Three words are carved into the basin's outer face, letters uneven and hand-cut, weathered to the color of old bone: IAM NOTUM SUM. Someone carved these and meant them. Someone sat where you are sitting now and pressed a blade to stone and spoke Latin into the rock like a prayer or a contract. The inscription does not explain itself. It does not need to.

The water is impossibly clear. The basin is chest-high. The rim is wide enough to sit on, and someone is already sitting there — a man, solidly built, stone visible at the shoulders and in the joints of his hands where they rest on the rim. Plain clothes. A face that has the quality of someone who arrived somewhere and decided to stay. He is looking at you with the expression of someone who has been waiting for a very long time and is not yet sure whether the waiting is over.

He does not speak first. The water does. It fills the square with its low, constant voice, and the man on the rim watches you with the patience of stone that has learned to wait.

You press your hand to the inscription. The letters are deep enough to feel under your fingertips — grooves worn smooth by other hands, other arrivals. The stone beneath them hums faintly, a vibration too low to hear but present, as if the words are less inscription than electrode.

*What is the conduit carrying underneath you?* That was your question when you spoke the words and meant them. The stone does not answer. But the man on the rim shifts his weight, and his eyes track to your hand on the inscription, and something in his face changes — a recognition, or the beginning of one.

"You're here," he says. Not a question.
