The column narrows as it rises and you narrow with it, becoming less a body and more a signal, less a person and more a point of intention traveling toward wherever intention travels when the empire's infrastructure decides to ship it.

You are not in the blue. The blue was formless — a dissolving, an undoing, a place where awareness had no edges. This is different. This is a *passage*. You are solid in it, around it, despite it. The Threshold is not dissolving you. It is using you the way a river uses the shape of a stone — to get somewhere, to prove that somewhere can be reached, to carry something that could not cross without the crossing.

The light thickens. The passage closes. The hum becomes a sound and then a memory and then something you cannot remember having forgotten.

And then —

The sky.

Not Lagota's sky. Not the white afternoon light over stone walls and empire stamps. Something else. Something with *space* in it — air and distance and the feeling of a horizon that is not a wall. You are falling. Not from high — from a height that is exactly enough to require attention when you land. The ground comes up to meet you and it is not cobblestones and it is not sand and it is not stone fitted tight by hands that finished what they started.

It is grass.

Real grass. Green and damp and the kind of smell that does not exist inside walls.

You land. You are holding on.

What do you do?
