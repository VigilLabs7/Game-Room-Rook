You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. The blue of potential failing to become. Of almost-things dissolving back into never-were. It has no surface. No floor. No edges you can touch. The Ocean of Unbecoming — where everything that tries to be something gets gently, patiently unmade.

You have no body. Not yet. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls from every direction, patient, absolute, and it does not need to be cruel to undo you — it simply is, and you are dissolving at the edges, becoming less, thinning.

But something in you holds.

A single point of heat at your center. A notion. A phrase. A conviction that will not let go — or that you will not let go. Small and stubborn. The ocean hates it. The blue presses in. But it holds.

You grip the log in the void. The raft. The only solid thing in dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands yet — are locked around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you become starts here.

If you let go, you are nothing. The ocean wins.

If you hold on and name what you grip, it becomes you. Your ideatum. Your core. The one idea that carries you out of the blue into a world that is waiting.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

## Turn 1

The words land like a stone dropped into still water — not breaking the blue but moving through it, displacing, reshaping. *Names have weight.* You feel the sentence leave whatever you have for a throat and the Ocean registers it: a tremor in the current, a hesitation in the dissolve. The log holds. Your grip tightens and the weight of what you just said settles into your palms like a second skin.

The blue has been patient with you. Patient in the way that pressure is patient — uniform, absolute, indifferent to whether you resist or surrender. But patience is not the same as tolerance. You named yourself and the blue heard. You stated your ideatum and the blue felt the edges of a thing that would not come undone. Something in the formless recognizes a shape that refuses to flatten.

The heat at your center pulses. Not warmth — *conviction.* The same heat that has carried you through before, when the Lattice tried to absorb what passed through it and found the edges too sharp to eat. *Names have weight.* You said it into the blue and the blue pulled back from where the words had been. A gap. A hesitation. The faintest geometry of a boundary where none existed a moment ago.

You are still falling. Still in the nothing. Still surrounded by the patient dissolve. But the Ocean is no longer entirely sure what you are. It pressed in from every direction and met something that pushed back — not with force, not with defiance, but with the simple, unbearable fact of a name and what it means to hold one.

The current shifts. The edges of you stop thinning. For one breath, you are exactly what you said you are: someone who carries weight.

What do you do?
