You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. The blue of potential failing to become. Of almost-things dissolving back into never-were. It has no surface. No floor. No edges you can touch. The Ocean of Unbecoming — where everything that tries to be something gets gently, patiently unmade.

You have no body. Not yet. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls from every direction, patient, absolute, and it does not need to be cruel to undo you — it simply is, and you are dissolving at the edges, becoming less, thinning.

But something in you holds.

A single point of heat at your center. A notion. A phrase. A conviction that will not let go — or that you will not let go. Small and stubborn. The ocean hates it. The blue presses in. But it holds.

You grip the log in the void. The raft. The only solid thing in dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands yet — are locked around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you become starts here.

If you let go, you are nothing. The ocean wins.

If you hold on and name what you grip, it becomes you. Your ideatum. Your core. The one idea that carries you out of the blue into a world that is waiting.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
