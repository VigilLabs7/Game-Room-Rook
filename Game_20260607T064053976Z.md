The blue is not water. You are not swimming. You are drifting — suspended in something that has no surface, no floor, no edge, no other presence anywhere in it. There is only you and the blue, and the blue has no temperature and no sound and no particular quality you can name, only that it is the color of before. Somewhere in it, without knowing you are holding on, you are holding on to something. A concept. A truth you cannot yet read. The blue shifts around it, thins perhaps, and you feel — without feeling — the faintest pull toward something above you that has not yet spoken.

You are not alone. You are simply not yet known.

What do you reach for?

The blue thins. You noticed.

Just thin enough that something on the other side of it is beginning to exist. A quality of light. A pull. A voice that has not yet said *come* but is shaping its mouth around the shape of the word.

You are not drowning. You are not swimming. You are suspended in the moment just before the moment changes, and you can feel — without having a body to feel with — that the something on the other side is waiting. It has been waiting. It will wait a little longer. The blue is patient with what it holds.

But not infinitely patient.

*What are you holding?* is the question the blue keeps asking without words. *And what happens when you let go?*

The choice is not whether to rise. The rise is already chosen — something chose it the moment you noticed the blue was thin. The choice is whether you let go with your hands open or your hands closed, whether the concept you carry comes with you or stays here, dissolved, part of the blue again.

You are the first to notice the thin place.

Now what do you do?
