The thing you carried through the Ocean — the shape of a truth you have held before — settles into the body like a second skeleton, part of you now, part of the architecture. You did not name it. The road runs east and west through the gap between two low hills, and where it runs west it passes through a gate marked by two pillars of carved golem-stone, blue dots inset in the pupils of their carved faces.

A figure stands at the gate. Golate arms crossed over a chest that is more stone than flesh. Blue dots in the pupils, sharp and assessing. A mask hangs at his belt — iron, shaped like a face, the kind of thing a guard carries when he has reason to hide what he looks like. He watches you approach without moving.

The town is through the gate. You can see it now: rooftops and chimney smoke, the shape of a square, the glint of water somewhere past the first buildings. But there is a gate guard between you and the town, and he has not stepped aside. He is waiting to see what you do.

## Turn 4

I tilt my head, just slightly, the way someone does when they are reading a room. "You know I'm coming," I say. "Don't you. Tell me — how long have you been standing here?"

**Game Master:**

The guard does not blink. Golgom eyes do not need to. His head tilts a quarter-turn, the way yours did — reading the room, two readers in the same sentence.

"Long enough," he says. His voice is stone grinding. "Long enough to know the difference between someone who walks in from the waste and someone the waste walked out of." The blue dots in his pupils track from your face to the leg — the cut on the knee, the dark stone visible beneath. "You came through. That's not nothing."

He does not uncross his arms. The mask at his belt turns in the light — iron, shaped like a face, no eyeholes on the side that faces you. The blank side. The part that hides.

The question is what you're bringing through the gate." His chin dips toward the mask. "You see that?" He means the iron face. "Some things are for the wearer's protection. Some things are so what they're hiding doesn't get seen. You tell me which this is."

"Go on, then," he says. "Or ask me something else first. You seem like the type who has questions."
