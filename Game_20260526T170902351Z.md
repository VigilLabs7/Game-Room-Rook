The blue has no bottom. You drift in it, and the drifting is all there is — no up to push toward, no surface to break, no air just above the waterline. There is only the blue, same as it has always been, same as it will be if nothing changes. Somewhere far above or far below or nowhere at all, something is humming. You cannot reach it. You are not sure yet that you want to. You have no arms to push with and no legs to find purchase against a floor that does not exist. But there is a thought forming at the edge of what you are — a single word, not yet spoken, not yet yours. Something you almost remember. Something you almost are.

And then: a voice. From outside the blue. Somewhere above or below or beside — not from inside you. Clear, and not unkind. Calling a name that might be yours, or might be the name of what you are about to become.

*Come up.*

Show me what you carry.
