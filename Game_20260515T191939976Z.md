You have no hands yet. You will. The blue is patient in a way that feels like cruelty but isn't — it simply has nothing invested in whether you continue. You are a tremor in a medium that has swallowed a thousand tremors before yours.

But you are still here. Still holding the thread.

Something warm pulses at your center. A word you haven't spoken yet. A shape you haven't worn. The blue presses in and the blue waits and you wait with it, because letting go is not something you do. Not yet. Not after everything it cost you to arrive here, at this precise point in the nothing, where something in you refuses to dissolve.

The surface is far above. You cannot see it. You can feel it — a faint bright pressure that might be a world, might be a promise, might be a trap. Others are rising with you. You cannot see them either. But they are there.

The blue presses in.

**What do you do to hold on?**
