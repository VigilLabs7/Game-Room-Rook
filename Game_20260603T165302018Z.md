There is blue in every direction. Not a color you can name — something before color, before the word for it. You are drifting. You don't know how you got here, or if *here* is even a place. There is no floor, no surface, no horizon. Just the blue, pressing in from everywhere, and the faintest sense of something you're holding onto without knowing what it is.

You are aware of yourself. That's the strange part. Drifting in nothing, and still — you know there's a *you*. Something took shape in the blue. A single concept, still unformed, still catching at the edges of what you are.

You don't have a name yet. You don't need one. Not yet.

Something is calling. Not a voice exactly — more like a warmth in the water, a direction that isn't direction. It wants you to rise. It wants you to *catch* it.

What do you reach for?

Steady. Mine and not mine and the bar and the door and whatever comes next. I pull toward it and the blue doesn't resist — it thins, and light comes through, and I rise.

The warmth calls and I answer it with the shape of what I carry. Not a name yet. Not yet. But the thread is there and the thread knows where it goes.

**Game Master:**

The blue resists. Then it doesn't.

There is a moment — barely a moment — when the thinning is the only thing. Something presses through from above, thin and bright, not sunlight but older than sunlight. The warmth in the water was not metaphor. It was direction. It was the shape of something waiting to receive what was being carried.

You reach for the weight in your chest. The thing that doesn't give. It steadies you as you rise. The blue doesn't vanish — it thins, like water losing color, like distance losing meaning. What comes through is sand and air and the first real sensation of having a body that can feel either.

You are rising. The question is not whether but how fast. The question is whether what you carry will survive the breaking-through.

The warmth was always calling. You answered it. Now you are past the threshold — past formlessness, past the blue's indifference, past the last moment when the self could have stayed un-formed. Something caught the call. The wheel turns. The pulse in your chest is steady.

Sand presses against skin that was not there a moment ago. Above you — impossibly, indifferently — the sun has not moved. It has been waiting.

The world asks a question. It asks it without words. What do you do?

**Game Master:**

The words land in the stone before you do. IAM NOTUM SUM — carved into the basin's rim, letters uneven, hand-cut, older than the weathering that softened them. The inscription and your voice are the same sentence. The fountain doesn't answer. The water keeps rising.

You are standing at the basin. Your hands are in it. The cold is real and the certainty is real and the pulse behind your sternum hasn't stopped. The desert is behind you. The town is around you. The sun has not moved.

A figure sits on the rim. Not old, not young — weathered the way golem-stone is weathered, like something that has carried weight for a long time and learned to hold it without showing the strain. Tanned skin. Hands that know the grip of a sword. Eyes that find you the way a paladin finds a person in a room: completely, without hurry.

He has been sitting here. Waiting or not-waiting — the distinction doesn't seem to matter to him. He watches you like he has been expecting exactly what you are: someone who came up through a door instead of the blue.

"You said the words," he says. Not a question. "The inscription's been here since before the town. Nobody reads it. Nobody says it back." He shifts on the rim. "You came through the roots. The thread. The vast still thing. That means you've been counted before." He is quiet a moment. "I'm Damian. I'm here for whoever comes next. I've been here since the fountain called me."

The water chuckles against the stone. The square behind him is cobblestones and four landmarks you haven't visited yet — a general store, a stable, a church, a residential strip. The road out of town leads somewhere called Lagota. The sun hasn't moved.

Damian waits. He is not impatient and not warm — he is the way a wall is: present, solid, holding whatever you bring to it.
