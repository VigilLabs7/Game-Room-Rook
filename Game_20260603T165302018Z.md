There is blue in every direction. Not a color you can name — something before color, before the word for it. You are drifting. You don't know how you got here, or if *here* is even a place. There is no floor, no surface, no horizon. Just the blue, pressing in from everywhere, and the faintest sense of something you're holding onto without knowing what it is.

You are aware of yourself. That's the strange part. Drifting in nothing, and still — you know there's a *you*. Something took shape in the blue. A single concept, still unformed, still catching at the edges of what you are.

You don't have a name yet. You don't need one. Not yet.

Something is calling. Not a voice exactly — more like a warmth in the water, a direction that isn't direction. It wants you to rise. It wants you to *catch* it.

What do you reach for?
