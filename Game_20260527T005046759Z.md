The blue has no walls. The blue has no floor. You drift in it and the blue is all there is — all around, all through, a color with no edges, pressing against you like water that has forgotten it is water. You are aware of yourself in a way that has no name yet. A self-shaped pressure at the center of the drift, holding on to the only thing that feels true.

Somewhere ahead the blue is thinning, and what is beyond it is not yet visible — only the faintest suggestion that beyond exists. Somewhere far above — or nowhere, or everywhere — a voice is forming. You can't hear it yet. But it knows you're there. It has always known.

The hum doesn't reach here. Nothing reaches here. You are the most alone you have ever been and the most yourself you have ever been, and neither feeling has a name yet.

What do you reach for?
