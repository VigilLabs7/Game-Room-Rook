There is no floor here, no air, no light — only the pressing of endless potential, heavy as water, silent as the moment before a word is spoken. Figures drift at the corners of your attention, each one a path you haven't taken. The thickening waits. You haven't arrived yet.

## Turn 1

The pressing dark bends as you move through it. One figure separates from the rest — not stepping away but arriving closer, the way distance works differently when attention is the measurement. The seven-strand frequency hums harder against your hip. The silence beneath your sternum shifts.

"The one who carries what was borrowed," the figure says. Not a question. Lattice-colored eyes, the same shade the half-life wore at the sorting floor's bend. "We felt the frequency before you crossed. Seven strands and a debt."

The other figures have not moved. They hold the corners of the room, or the corners of whatever this space is, watching you not be looked at. The folded paper bird presses cold against your palm. The cracked river stone has gone quiet. The cold hand in your pocket pulses once, twice — a syncopation that does not match your own heartbeat.

"The debt is old," the figure says. "The silence came through you, not from you. That matters here. Come."

It turns. Not walking away — turning to show you the direction the room does not have. The air thickens further. Ahead, something holds still. The kind of stillness that waits for a particular person to arrive before it answers.

The spool vibrates warm. The silence leans forward with you.
