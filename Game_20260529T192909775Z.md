There is only blue. You are suspended in it and the suspending is the whole of existence — no floor to push off from, no surface to break toward, nothing in any direction but the same submerging color. You are aware, which is more than nothing, but the awareness has no home yet, no name, no edges you could point to. You are clinging to something. The only thing keeping you from dissolving into the blue entirely. You don't know what it is. You only know that the grip is everything. Somewhere outside the blue, above it, a voice speaks — casual, warm, like someone who has done this before and found it worth doing again. The words land near you. The thing you are holding shivers. The blue thins at the margins of what you are, and you start to become more solid around it.

What do you do?
