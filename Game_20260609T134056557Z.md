Blue. Not a color you'd call blue if you didn't have the word for it — just the sense of being held inside something without walls, without a bottom, without a surface. You drift. There is no direction to drift in except the idea of direction. You have no body here, not yet, only the shape of where one might be when this is over. But something is catching in the blue — a single concept, faint, becoming less faint. You don't know what you're holding until you feel it catch on something outside yourself.

A voice. From above. Somewhere outside all of this.

What do you hold onto?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold the words like a rail in the dark. *IAM NOTUM SUM.* The shape of what was buried and waiting steadies me — the second heartbeat, the truth that cannot be killed. Someone else said these words before me and came through. I say them now and the blue thins. I go toward the voice.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.
Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen.
