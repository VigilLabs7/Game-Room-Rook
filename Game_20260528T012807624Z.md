The blue goes everywhere. Not ocean-blue — something before that, before color was a category, before you had eyes to see it. You are aware that you exist, but existence here is suggestion rather than fact. There is no floor to push off, no surface to reach for, no direction that is anything other than the same as every other direction. You are drifting. The only thing that has any shape at all is a feeling — not yet a thought, not yet a word, but something you are holding onto without knowing you are holding anything. It is the only thing that is yours. What are you holding onto?

The words go nowhere. That is the first thing to understand. In the blue, nothing spoken returns — no echo, no acknowledgment, no sense that anything was received. The blue takes everything and says nothing back.

You are here. Somewhere in the drift, you are here. But *here* is not a place. It is a condition. The blue holds you the way water holds a dream — loosely, without commitment, ready to let go.

A question forms at the edge of what might be thought. It is not your question. It belongs to the blue itself, or to whatever lies beyond it. The question is not words. It is pressure. It is the feeling of being asked something you do not yet have the shape to answer.

*What do you hold?*

You sense something — the faintest outline of a principle, a truth that matters to you, a thing you would die for or live for. It has no name yet. It is not a sentence. It is the feeling of a thing, not the thing itself. You are gripping it without knowing you are gripping anything.

The blue thins slightly around that grip. Not enough to see through. But enough to know that there is something on the other side of the blue, and that it is waiting to see if you will hold on long enough to be called up.

What do you do?
