The blue has no bottom. The blue has no surface. You are drifting in it — or you are the drifting, it's hard to tell the difference yet. There is no current because there is no water. There is no cold because there is no body. There is only the faint sense of a shape you are holding onto without knowing you are holding anything at all.

Somewhere above — or it might be below, or it might be a direction that doesn't exist yet — something is humming. You can't hear it. You feel it the way you might feel a word on the tip of your tongue, if you had a tongue, if you had a you.

You are not nothing. You are not yet something. You are the space between, and it is very, very blue.

A name surfaces. Not yours — not yet. A name you once heard, or half-heard, or made up in the half-second before you forgot you made it. You cling to it. It is the only shape in the blue.

Something is calling. You can't make out the words. You don't know if you want to.

**What do you do?**
