The blue is everywhere. Not the blue of water, not the blue of sky — the blue of before. Of almost. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a shape in fog: present, but not yet committed to existing. There is no surface. There is no floor. There is nothing to push against and nothing to reach for, and yet — something catches. A concept. A single notion you're clinging to without knowing what you're holding, the way a drowning person might grip a piece of wood without knowing it's saving them.

You are the log. You are the thing clinging to the log. You don't know which is which.

Then — from above, from somewhere outside the blue entirely — a voice. Not calling your name. Not yet. Calling the shape of you: *Come up, darling.* Or not darling. Or something else entirely. The words land somewhere in the blue and the blue shivers and something in you begins to rise.

What do you hold onto?

## Turn 1

**Player:** Intriguing imagery. A blank canvas, perhaps, ripe for the imposition of will. Let us hope that whatever takes shape upon this "almost" reflects compassion, justice, and reason, as the Seven Tenets guide us. The alternative, as history demonstrates, is often quite bleak.

**Game Master:**

The words leave you and the blue answers nothing — but it *hears.*

Something shifts in the quality of the everywhere-blue. A change in pressure, like the moment before a sound becomes audible. The voice above is still happening, still not yours, but it is closer now and the words within it have begun to separate. You can almost hear individual syllables. Not directed at you. Directed near you, the way light falls on a surface that is not the source.

You are still in it. Still holding the log — your concept, your reason, the thing you are clinging to without knowing its name yet. The blue has not let go. But it has thinned around the place where you are holding on, and the thinning is not nothing.

What reached you was the idea of fairness. The notion that some things ought to be otherwise, and that the otherwise matters more than the comfortable. You did not choose it. You arrived already carrying it, the way a room arrives already carrying its temperature.

The voice above sharpens. Not calling. Not yet. But *nearing.*

What do you do?
