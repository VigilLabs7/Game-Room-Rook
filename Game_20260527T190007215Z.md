The blue is all there is. Not blue the way sky is blue — not a color you look at. Blue the way water is a place you are in, the way pressure has a temperature. You drift. You are aware of drifting, which means you are aware of yourself, which means something in you is still holding on.

There is nothing to hold with. No floor, no surface, no edge to push off from. Just the blue, and the faint sense that you are almost something — that a shape is forming around the thing you don't yet have a name for.

Then a voice. Somewhere above or outside or beside the blue. It says your name, or it says *come up*, or it says nothing at all and just reaches. The ideatum responds before you do. Something catches.

What do you reach for?
