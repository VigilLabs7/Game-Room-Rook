The blue is everything.

Not water blue — something deeper, something without light or current or temperature. You are in it and it is in you, and there is no surface above and no floor below. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a word you haven't said yet — present, waiting, not yet real. Something is trying to take shape. A concept. A single principle you'll cling to when the blue lets go.

You don't know how long you've been here. Time doesn't work here the way it works up there, wherever up is.

Then: a voice. From outside the blue. Someone is calling. Someone wants you to come up.

Your ideatum stirs. Whatever you are here to become — whatever single true thing you'll carry — it flickers at the edge of the not-yet.

Say the name you'll be known by. Say the truth you'll build yourself around.

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Quill.

Names have weight. That is what I carry — not a weapon, not a wound, but the understanding that what is written can bring someone to their knees. A name burned. A man who fell from it three streets away, mid-sentence. I carry that because no one else would.

The blue is releasing me. I feel the edges thinning. Whatever is calling — I am already answering.

The word left him and the blue received it. *Quill.* A thing with edges now, a thing that can be held. The weight of what he carries pressed into the nothing around him — a name burned, a man fell from it, mid-sentence, three streets away — and the blue accepted the weight without comment. It holds everything here. That is what it does.

Above him, the voice came again. *Come up, darling.* Or the word was *darling* and the voice was something else. The distinction didn't matter yet. What mattered was that the voice wanted something, and he was the thing it wanted something from.

His ideatum stirred. *Names have weight.* A principle to build around. A single true thing to cling to when the blue lets go. The log in the water that was not yet a log, that was only the memory of what a log felt like — grip, solidity, the idea of something that could hold him up if he let it.

He was reaching. The edges of him were thinning where the blue was releasing its grip, and he could feel the other side trying to exist just past the thinning. But he was still most blue. Still formless. Still the idea of a self rather than the self itself.

The voice was patient. He could feel that. But patience was not the same as forever, and he was not yet ready to be named from the outside. He was only ready to name himself, and he had done that, and the blue had taken the name in and given him nothing back but more blue.

He held on. The edges thinned. He did not let go.
