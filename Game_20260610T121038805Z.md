The blue has no end. It has no bottom either — you know this because you have already tried to find it, tried to push down through the nothing, and the nothing was all there was. You are here. You are formless. You are holding something and you don't yet know what it is — only that if you let go, you will dissolve. Somewhere above you can feel a pull, like a hand reaching down through miles of water. You don't know who it belongs to. You don't know what happens if you take it. But you know you are running out of blue.

What do you do?
