You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution.

Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer of existence where things have weight and light and names. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
