The blue is everything. No surface, no floor, no horizon — only the same blue in every direction, and you in it, drifting. Something is taking shape, faint at first, becoming more definite: a concept you are gripping without yet knowing you are gripping anything. You are alone here. Every drifter is. The conduit hum does not reach this deep. What are you holding onto? What truth do you carry, even now, before you have a name for it?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold the names. What was done, and by whom, and to whom. The record of the room where people did not come out. The columns must balance — I am the weight that balances them.

**Game Master:**

The words catch. You feel them land — not in the blue, but in the shape of the thing you're gripping. The columns. The balance. The weight. It was always yours, you realize. You were always carrying it. You just didn't know what shape it had until you said it.

The blue thins.

And then there is the pressure. Air where there was none, and the sudden weight of it on skin you didn't know you had. Sand, fine and hot, shifting under hands that are new and not-new at once — the hands of a body that knows what it is for. Your knees hit the surface. Your palms flatten against it, catching you. Keeping you above the drift.

You are not drowning. You are not surfacing. You are *here*, on the edge — the sand warm and the sun impossibly high and the whole desert holding its breath around you like a room where something terrible has just finished happening.

You are holding on.

The weight is still in your chest. The columns. The names. The record. You don't know what room. You don't know whose names. But you know you are the weight that balances them, and you know the columns will not balance themselves, and you know — with the certainty of something that has always been true — that you cannot let them fall.

The sand shifts. The sun doesn't move.

What are you holding onto?
