The blue is everything. You drift in it — no body to weigh you down, no edges to define where you end and the water begins. Somewhere in the nothing, a single thought takes shape: something you are gripping without knowing you are gripping anything at all. A voice cuts through the nothing — not from beside you, not from below, but from somewhere outside the blue entirely. Your concept answers being seen, and you begin to rise. The blue thins. Something on the other side is pulling.

You break through.

What do you hold?

IAM NOTUM SUM.

But the words find nothing to catch. Not here — not in the blue. You are not yet a known thing. The phrase falls into the water and the water takes it and does not answer.

A figure on the fountain's rim. Watching. A paladin — golem stone laced through the ribs and spine, visible beneath sun-dark skin. Hands weathered. The kind of stillness that knows how to wait. He has not spoken. His eyes track the evidence across the stones and then rise to your face.

The water spills over the basin's lip in a continuous, silver murmur.

Your first full breath since the courthouse stairs fills your lungs. Tin and dust and the faint sweetness of water that shouldn't exist in a place this dry. You are standing. You are holding nothing now. Everything you carried is open on the cobblestones between you and the figure on the fountain's edge — and he is still watching.
