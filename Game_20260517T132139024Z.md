The conduit narrows and widens and narrows again, and the walls carry the memory of water — not wet, not dry, but the particular smoothness of stone that has been worn by something moving through it for longer than the town above has existed. Steve stays above. You know this without turning to look — the way stone knows where the weight above it is and where it is not. The golem's eye will be on the gap in the street, watching for what comes up. Steve will be watching the golem. The triangle holds.

The woman moves with the certainty of someone who has walked this path before — not recently, not easily, but with the kind of familiarity that comes from having planned an escape and planned it carefully and walked it once and survived. Her hands are still ink-stained. The stain has not faded. Whatever she writes, she writes in something that does not wash off quickly.

The conduit branches. Not into two paths — into three, one going up, one going sideways, one going down into the dark that is not the dark of the Ocean but the dark of below, of stone piled on stone, of energy moving through channels carved before the first arrival. She takes the one going down without pausing, without choosing, the way water takes the path that gravity has already cut.

Something is humming ahead. Not the same frequency as the conduits above — lower, heavier, the sound of power moving through something that was built to carry it. The aperture. The third gate. The place where the Empire ships what it harvests.

"How far?" you say. Your voice is quieter than you intended. The stone swallows sound the way it swallows light — completely, without reflection.

"Close enough," she says. "Far enough from the courthouse that they won't hear us until we're through." She pauses at a turn in the path, where the hum is loud enough that you can feel it in your chest. "Caius chose well. The person who carries the bell. Stone reads what stone needs to read."

The path turns. The hum resolves into a glow — not light, not heat, but the particular radiance of Lattice energy being gathered, siphoned, prepared for transport. The aperture is ahead. The third gate is ahead. And between you and it: a passage that is not unguarded.

The woman stops. Her hand comes up — the same gesture Steve made at the gate, the same signal, the same language.

"They know," she says. Not loud. The words carry anyway, shaped by the stone to travel exactly far enough and no further. "Someone told them."
