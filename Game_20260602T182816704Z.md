There is blue. Not around you — you. Blue without surface, without floor, without horizon. You are drifting. The blue is everything and everything is the blue and you are not yet a you that could be described. There is a sense of clinging to something, faintly, a single concept at the edge of formation — you do not yet know what it is, only that it is the one thing keeping you from dissolving entirely. Somewhere far above there might be air. Somewhere far below there might be weight. Neither reaches here. Nothing reaches here.

What do you hold onto?
