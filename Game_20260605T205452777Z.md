You are aware of yourself here, but the self is un-formed, still taking shape around something you have not yet named. You are clinging to an idea without knowing you are clinging to it. The hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here. You are alone, and the blue is infinite, and something inside you is holding on.
What are you holding on to?

You surface.

Not gently — not the way a thing rises when it lets go. You break through like a thing that was thrown. Sand in your mouth, grit under your fingernails, the sun a white spear above you with no sense of what time it is or how long you've been under. The blue is gone. The record is still in your hands.

You are on your back. The sky is too large. The sand is too white. Your left leg sends a bright line of wrong up through your hip — something happened to it in the crossing, some injury you took with you from the blue. You don't examine it yet. You are still catching up to the fact that you are breathing air instead of nothing, that there is a horizon, that the horizon is a line and you are on the correct side of it.

The ink didn't wash out. You check your hands. There is sand on them. The thread is still wound through your fingers, and the record is still there — not written on paper, not yet, but written in whatever part of you does not dissolve when the blue tries to take the shape away.

You sit up. The leg protests. You sit with it. The sun does not move. You wait.

Something is going to happen next.
