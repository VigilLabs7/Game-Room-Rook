The blue has no end and no floor and no surface. You drift in it and the drift is all there is — not cold, not warm, not anything except the blue itself, pressing in from every direction at once. You are aware that you are, but the you that is aware has no edges yet, no name, no weight. Something is taking shape in the drift. A concept. A single, stubborn fact that you are holding onto without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The blue does not hurry. There is no time here, not really — only the slow accumulation of whatever you are becoming. You might feel a pull from above, a voice calling through the thickness of the water, or you might not. That is not up to you. The blue is patient and the blue is all there is until it isn't, and the way out is the same as the way in: something true that you cannot let go of.

What do you reach for?
