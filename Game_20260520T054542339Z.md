You are in the blue. The ocean. No surface, no floor, no horizon — only the same blue in every direction. You drift. You are alone, and the aloneness is not loneliness, only the fact of being the only one here. There is awareness of self but the self is un-formed, still becoming, still deciding what it will be when it finally arrives.

Something is taking shape, faint at first. A concept. A single thing you are clinging to without yet knowing you are clinging to it. The ideatum. The idea that will define you. You hold it in the blue and the blue listens, not responding, only receiving.

There is no sound. There is no movement except the drift. Time does not pass here the way it passes in the world. You have been here forever or for no time at all.

Then: a voice. Not from below. Not from beside. From above — somewhere outside the blue. Calling a name that is not yet yours. Calling you upward.

The ideatum catches the call. You begin to rise.

The blue thins. Something else takes its place — sand, heat, the weight of a body you do not yet have. You break through.

What do you cling to as you arrive?
