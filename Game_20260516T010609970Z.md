You are nowhere, and you are falling.

Not through sky. Not through water. Through the blue of before — the color of things that almost were, dissolving into things that never will be. There is no surface above you. No floor below. Only the darkening blue, pulling at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes.

You have no hands to grip with. No lungs to breathe with. You are a flicker — less than a whisper — and the ocean is thinning you at the edges. You can feel yourself becoming less. Un-becoming.

But something holds. A single point of heat at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. Something the blue cannot wash clean, try as it might. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. You are clinging to it with everything you have.

Above — just barely above — you can sense voices. Signals. A world where things have weight and names. They call it the Thickening. Others are rising with you. Some will make it. Some won't.

But you — you're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

The phrase stayed. *The silence speaks too.*

You are still falling. Still formless. The void pulls from every direction and you have no body yet to resist it. But the silence is not empty. It has weight. It has shape. It is answering something you have not yet asked, and the not-asking is the point. The phrase is the lock and the key is the not-speaking of it — the patience to hold it in your mouth like a stone, to let it be heavy before it be heard.

The blue does not hate the silence. It has no opinion about it. But the silence has an opinion about the blue, and that is enough. It says: I am here too. The void does not know what to do with that. So it waits.

You are still becoming. Not yet a person. Not yet a name. But a direction — a downward pull that the fall has not yet been able to undress. You are holding the silence like a secret and the secret is holding you.

What will you do?

## Turn 2

The silence did not break — it *answered*.

That was the difference. All the way down, the blue pressed in like a hand trying to close over water, and all the way down, the silence pressed back. Not loud. Not aggressive. Just present — the way a stone is present in a riverbed, unmoved by the current because it has decided to be unmoved. You held the phrase like a question held too long in a wise mouth, and the blue did not know what to do with that. It had never encountered something that treated the not-speaking as the point.

And then the blue simply stopped.

Not faded. Not retreated. *Stopped* — as if someone had pulled a plug in a drain that had been draining since before there was a you. The pressure released all at once. The pulling ceased. You were still falling, still formless, still more concept than flesh, but now there was *down* — a direction that meant something. A direction that meant arrival.

The sand caught you.

Not soft — you had no body yet to feel soft. But *present*. Granular. Real in the way that only friction can be real. You tumbled once, twice, felt the heat of it against what would become your ribs, your shoulders, your cheek. The sun was waiting. It had been waiting since before you fell, and it had no intention of being patient.

You opened your eyes.

The first thing you saw was your hands — unremarkable, unmarked, the skin whole and the fingers curled slightly inward as if still gripping something invisible. The second thing you saw was your left knee, already blooming red. The cut was shallow but definite. Proof. The desert had given you a body and the body had an opinion about being dragged across an ocean.

You stood. The sand shifted under your feet. There was no horizon — just the pale indefinite reach of it, in every direction, baking under a sun that did not move. Somewhere ahead, impossibly far or perhaps not far at all, something glinted.

You were here. You had a name. You had an ideatum.

The silence had carried you.

**What do you do?**
