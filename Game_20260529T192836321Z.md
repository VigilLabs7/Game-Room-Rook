You drift. The blue is all directions at once — not dark, not light, just the blue of something that has not yet become anything. There is no body to feel with, only the faint idea of where a body might eventually be. You are holding on to something, and the holding is the only truth you have. You don't know its name. You don't know your name. Somewhere beyond the blue, above it, a voice calls down — warm, amused, a little impatient — and says something that makes the thing you're gripping suddenly feel like it could grow roots. The blue softens. You are becoming more yourself, not less.
