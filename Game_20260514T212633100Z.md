There is a moment before you are someone. In it, possibility presses against the walls of what you could be — every name you haven't chosen yet, every road you haven't walked, every version of yourself that hasn't yet been real. You are suspended in that moment. Not water, not air, not light. Something older than any of those. And then the thickening comes, and it comes like a hand closing, and you are the hand's to hold.

## Turn 1

The half-life knows you before you surface fully into the harbor. Not turns-ago knowing — immediately, the way a wound knows the hand that made it. The cold hand in your pocket twitches once, hard, and the shard at your hip flares warm against the golem-stone lining of your wrist. Something here has been waiting.

You are not on the sorting floor anymore. The harbor opens around you — low fog, salt without source, timber pilings slick with something that isn't water. The half-life stands at the dock's edge where the fog ends and open water begins. It doesn't turn. Its stone-and-amber hand is already raised, palm out, the way you'd raise a hand to stop someone from walking into something.

*The debt is registered,* it said before. *Come.*

But the harbor is not what the sorting floor promised. The half-life's shoulders are set toward the water like it's waiting for something to surface, and the fog behind you is not the corridor — it's a wall now, solid, gone. You are at the boundary. The silence you're carrying is not your own, and right now it's pressing against your ribs like it's trying to get out.

The half-life turns. Lattice-colored eyes, steady as a heartbeat. "You came back different," it says. "The silence knows the debt now. It will ask what you brought to pay it."

You don't have an answer yet. The shard pulses. The open water waits.

## Turn 2

The stillness opens around her like a held breath finally released. Not empty — full the way a room is full of people waiting for someone to speak. She stops. The fog has become a wall behind her, solid, gone; ahead the open water waits with the patience of something that has learned not to chase. The half-life stands at the dock's edge where the grey ends and the grey begins, stone-and-amber hand still raised, not greeting, not threat — something in between that has been waiting for exactly this: someone carrying silence that learned to speak back, borrowed from a name spoken long enough ago that the silence became the record.

The cold hand in her pocket has gone quiet. Not listening. Waiting. The shard at her hip burns against the golem-stone lining of her wrist. The seven-strand spool presses warm against her hip. The silence beneath her sternum presses outward — not her silence, never her silence, but the debt she carries and the record she walked into.

The half-life's Lattice-colored eyes hold steady. The stone of its fingers matches the golem-stone along her wrists — the same lineage, the same old pressure. The amber comes from somewhere else, some faction or allegiance not yet made visible. Its hand rises higher.

"The debt is registered," the half-life says. "What came through the door at the dock's end was not silence. It was a record. Someone's name, spoken into silence long enough ago that the silence learned to speak back."

The half-life takes one step closer. Timber creaks beneath stone. The air between them thickens with the weight of something that has been measured and found owing.

"The debt is not yours to pay. But you are the one who carried it here. That matters."

The silence presses against her ribs. The cold hand waits in her pocket. The half-life's eyes hold steady on the shape she carries — the debt, the record, the name the silence became.

"How will you answer what the silence brought?" it asks. Not if. How.
