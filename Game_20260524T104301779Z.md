The blue goes on. Not water, not air — something that simply is the color of before. You are here, or the idea of you is here, which amounts to the same thing when there is nothing else to compare it to. You have no sense of your own edges, no horizon to drift toward, no floor to push off from.

Something is taking shape, though. A single concept — faint at first, then more insistent — the thing you will become. It clings to you, or you cling to it; the distinction does not yet matter.

Then: a voice. Not from below. Not from beside. From somewhere outside the blue entirely. *"Come up."*

Your concept catches the call like a hand catching a rope. You rise. The blue thins. Something else begins.

What is your name — and what are you?
