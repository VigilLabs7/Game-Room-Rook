The blue is everything. You drift in it — or it drifts in you, and the distinction doesn't hold. There is no surface, no floor, no sound except the one you bring with you, the one you've been gripping since before you had a shape to grip with. You are aware of yourself, but the self is still catching on something faint and becoming more definite — a concept, a single principle you are holding without knowing you are holding anything. A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue. From above. Not calling your name — calling what you are.

What do you carry?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Quill. My ideatum is *Names have weight.* I carry three names in a ledger, and the weight of each one is different. One man I carry because no one else will. One I carry because it asked. One I carry because it is trying to get back to what it was before the aperture brought it through wrong. My fingers are stained with ink. I speak softly. The stone is always running along my right arm.

The words leave you and the blue does not answer.

You said your name. You said the thing you are for. The saying moved something — you felt it, a small heat in a place that did not have heat a moment ago — but the blue stayed the blue. You are still in it, still mostly of it, holding onto the part that is not it. The log under your hands is yours. It has not become driftwood.

Above you, just barely, a voice is happening. Not addressed to you. *Happening near you.* The way conversation in another room is happening — sound without shape, intent without aim. You can hear that there is a conversation. You cannot yet hear what it is about.

The three names in your ledger are each a different weight. You know this the way you know the pressure of your own fingers. One wanted forward. Two wanted back. The stone along your right arm has always run cooler than the rest of you, and you have always known that was for a reason.

You are not at the surface. You have not yet risen. You are barely holding on.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I am Quill.

My ideatum is *Names have weight.*

I grip the log. The cool stone along my right arm answers the blue. I listen for the shape of the voice above — not the words, but what it wants.

The voice comes again. Not closer — not distant the way distance means anything here — but *clearer*. As if whatever sits above the blue is leaning in to hear you think. You grip the log. The stone along your arm answers: cool, solid, the weight of something that has held other names before you. The blue presses in from every side. There is no up. There is no surface. There is only the log, and the voice, and the shape taking hold in your chest that is not yet a word but is becoming one.

You listen for what it wants. The answer comes not in language but in the thinning of the blue directly above you — a brightness that is not light, that is more like the memory of light, like the idea of warmth a body carries before the body exists to feel it. The voice did not call you darling. It called you *Quill*. The name settles into the space where the ideatum is still forming, and something in you recognizes the fit: names have weight, and this one is heavy enough to hold.

The log does not sink. It has never sunk. It bobs at the exact depth where you left it — which is nowhere, which is the same as everywhere in this blue. You are not rising. You are not falling. You are being *considered*. The voice above has seen you and is deciding what to make of what it saw.

The stone in your arm hums. Not the hum that reaches the Lattice — a different hum. Older. The hum of something that has been held in stone before.

**What do you do?**
