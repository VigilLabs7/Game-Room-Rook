The blue is everything. You drift in it — or it drifts in you, and the distinction doesn't hold. There is no surface, no floor, no sound except the one you bring with you, the one you've been gripping since before you had a shape to grip with. You are aware of yourself, but the self is still catching on something faint and becoming more definite — a concept, a single principle you are holding without knowing you are holding anything. A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue. From above. Not calling your name — calling what you are.

What do you carry?

Above you, just barely, a voice is happening. Not addressed to you. *Happening near you.* The way conversation in another room is happening — sound without shape, intent without aim. You can hear that there is a conversation. You cannot yet hear what it is about.

The three names in your ledger are each a different weight. You know this the way you know the pressure of your own fingers. One wanted forward. Two wanted back. The stone along your right arm has always run cooler than the rest of you, and you have always known that was for a reason.

You are not at the surface. You have not yet risen. You are barely holding on.

What do you do?
