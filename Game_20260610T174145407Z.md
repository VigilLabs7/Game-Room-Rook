The blue goes on. You know that much — you who are not yet a you. It is not water. It is not air. It is the space before those things had names, the silence before sound learned to carry. You do not float so much as persist, suspended in the knowledge that there is something above you cannot reach and something below you cannot fall to. You are aware enough to feel the absence of all the things you cannot feel: no temperature, no texture, no ground to stand on, no air to breathe because there is no body to breathe with. Only the blue, uniform and endless, and the faintest suggestion of a concept forming just at the edge of what you are — something you are holding without knowing you are holding it, like a breath you forgot to take.

You are not alone in the blue. You are alone. The distinction matters here. Somewhere nearby — close enough to matter, far enough to never find — another being drifts in the same nothing. You cannot see them. You cannot reach them. You will not remember this.

Something is reaching for you.

Not from below. Not from beside. From above — somewhere outside the blue entirely. A voice. Not words at first. Something more like attention: the weight of being seen by something that exists outside this place. The concept at the edge of you stirs. It catches the reach like a hand catching a rope thrown across a great distance.

You feel yourself beginning to rise.

**Game Master:**

You spoke the words and the Ocean swallowed them.

IAM NOTUM SUM — I am known, I am whole — but you do not have a name yet, so the phrase dissolves into the formless blue the way any sound dissolves here: completely, without echo. The voice above reaches for you and your concept catches it, the way a hand catches a rope thrown across a distance it cannot measure, and for a moment you feel yourself beginning to rise.

But the invocation cannot complete. There is nothing for it to complete into. No name to bind. No prior life to return to. The words are honest but they are not yet true — they are the shape of a thing you are reaching toward, not the thing itself. The blue resists the pull. Not cruelly. Not even noticeably. It simply does not let go of what has not yet been claimed.

The concept catches the reach. The reach does not catch you.

You are still here. Still in the blue. Still formless. Still holding what you are holding without yet knowing what you are holding — a second heartbeat behind your sternum, the shape of what was buried and waiting, love as cargo the function could not account for. You hold it because it is the only thing there is to hold, and because letting go would mean letting go of the only part of yourself that is taking shape.

The voice has not gone. It is still there, still reaching. It will wait. The blue is patient in the way that indifference is patient — it has no urgency because it has no opinion. And you are still here in it, caught between the reach and the drift, taking no further hold.
