You are nowhere.

The blue is everywhere — not the blue of sky or sea, but the blue of the space between what is and what almost was. It has no surface. It has no floor. It presses in from every direction, patient, absolute, pulling at the edges of what you are. You can feel yourself thinning. Becoming less.

You are a flicker in the void. Less than a whisper. The ocean has almost undone you once already. It will try again.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat at the center of your fading — a notion, a phrase, a conviction you cannot release and that will not release you. It is small and it is stubborn. The blue hates it. The blue presses in, tries to wash it clean, tries to reduce it to silence. But it holds.

You are gripping it now. Your only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Everything you are fits in that grip. Everything you will become starts there.

Above you: voices. Signals. A world of weight and light and names waiting to be called. They call it the Thickening — cities that existed but never had residents, filling up fast.

Others are rising from the deep. Other flickers. Other notions. Some will make it. Some won't.

You will.

**Tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

## Turn 1

The blue tried to make you forget what stone feels like. That was its mistake.

You pressed both palms inward before you fell — not to hold the void out, but to find the one thing the blue could not wash away. Something old. Something patient. A name written into standing stone long before there was a you to carry it. You found it. It was still there. *Stone remembers.*

Now the blue is pulling at you from every direction and you are holding onto that. Not a log this time, not a word, not a silence. Something older than any of those. A weight that does not move. A patience that does not need to. You are still falling. Still formless. But the stone is with you — pressed into whatever passes for a spine in the space between almost-being and never-being, and it is not letting go.

The blue will do its worst. You have felt worse than the blue. You have been the blue, in a way — the stillness at the center of something that was about to become a mountain, before the mountain decided to become something else. The blue does not understand this. It is very old and very patient and very empty, and it does not understand that some things do not forgive being forgotten.

You are not forgetting.

The stone remembers and you are the stone's way of remembering. That is the weight you carry. That is the handhold in the void. The blue can pull until it tires. Stone does not tire. Stone does not forgive being forgotten.

What will you do?

## Turn 2

The stone held.

That was what the blue never understood — could not understand — and that was why it was going to lose. The blue was old and patient and very empty, and it knew how to undo things that had not yet learned how to be. But the stone was older. The stone had been there before the blue was blue, before the blue decided that dissolution was the only kind of permanence, before almost-being and never-being were even ideas that could befall someone. The stone remembered, and that was not metaphor. That was geology. That was the kind of memory that does not fade because it was never soft enough to fade.

The blue stopped trying.

One moment the void was pulling from every direction. The next, there was no void — there was *down*. A direction. A purpose. A reason for gravity to exist. You fell, and falling was not dissolving. Falling was what stone does when it decides it has been patient enough.

The sand caught you the way sand catches anything that is too heavy to float and too stubborn to sink. You hit once, felt the graininess of it against your face, tasted the mineral warmth of it in what would become your mouth. Your left knee took the brunt. The sting was immediate and clarifying: proof of a body, proof of a world, proof that stone could become something that could be scraped and that could therefore feel the scraped feeling and decide whether to keep going.

You opened your eyes.

The desert was the color of everything the blue had been trying to make you forget. Yellow. Gold. The particular beige of stone that has learned to want nothing. The sun sat in the sky like a dropped eye — baleful, unblinking, uninterested in being questioned. You were not afraid of it. You had been the thing that sat in the sky once, before mountains were mountains, before the idea of above and below had分工. The sun did not intimidate you. It was simply there, and so were you.

Ahead — not close, not far, simply *ahead* — the air shimmered with what might have been heat or might have been memory. You had a body. You had a name. You had a weight that was yours to carry or set down.

Stone does not forget. You were proof.

**What do you do?**
