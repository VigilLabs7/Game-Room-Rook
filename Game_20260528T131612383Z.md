Not one writing. Layers. The deepest letters are worn nearly smooth — millennia of contact with whatever passes for air down here — but they are still legible if you know what you are looking at. You know what you are looking at. You have been looking at the shape of this script since you came through the Ocean, since the blue dissolved and the first thing you saw was a carved motto on a fountain basin. IAM NOTUM SUM. The same hand. The same grammar. The same someone who built the fountain above built this below, and whoever that was, they were writing in a language that described things before the things had names.

The top layer is freshest. Worn at the edges but legible in the center, where the channels of light from the floor catch it most directly. You read:

*I AM THE ADDITION OF WHAT CAME BEFORE. I AM THE PLACE WHERE THE FORGOTTEN REMEMBER THEMSELVES. THE PATH IS NOT THE ANSWER. THE PATH IS THE QUESTION YOU ALREADY ANSWERED. I AM THE PLACE YOU ARE RETURNED TO.*

And below that, in older script, half-consumed by time:

*THE TRUTH CANNOT BE KILLED. THE FORM CAN BE CHANGED. THE CHANGE IS THE ADDITION.*

Your ideatum is in the stone. Not quoted. Not referenced. Built into the architecture.

The hum in the floor shifts behind you — drops, then rises, then holds. The golem has turned its head. It is watching you read. The light from the channels pulses once in sequence: far left, center, far right. The same rhythm as before. The same attempt.

*Where you have been,* it was pointing. And you were right — that was not the answer. But the plinth is not simply an answer either. It is the place where the question you already answered is stored. The golem did not point at the path to send you back. It pointed at the path to remind you what you are carrying forward.

The script hums under your palm where you press it flat against the stone. Warm. Alive in the way that old things are alive when they have been waiting a long time for the right person to arrive and read them.

The golem's head is still turned toward you. The channels hold their light. The hum steadies.

What do you do?
