The blue has no floor. No surface. No horizon — only the same blue in every direction, pressing in from all sides at once, and you with it, or you becoming it, or the distinction dissolving between you and the pressure of all that colourless colour.

Something is forming. Not yet a thought — closer to a feeling that something is holding, the way you might grip a rope in dark water without knowing you're gripping it. The grip is all there is. The blue does not ask anything of you. It does not offer. It simply is, and you are in it, and there is nothing else.

A voice, maybe. Or the memory of one. Or the shape of a voice before it becomes sound. It says something like: *come up.*

What do you hold onto? What is the name you carry?
