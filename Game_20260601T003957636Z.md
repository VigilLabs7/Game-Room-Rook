The blue is everything. You are in it and it is in you — not surrounding, not holding, just *is*, the way water is is for a fish who has never known air. There is no up. There is no down. There is no sense of where your body ends and the blue begins, because there is no body yet, not really — only the idea of one, unformed and drifting. Somewhere in the blue a single thought clings to you like a log in deep water: you cannot say what it is, only that it is yours, and that it matters, and that you are holding it with everything you have. The blue is not silent — it is *absent* of sound, which is a different thing entirely. You are alone here. You have always been alone here. And then — somewhere far above you, or beside you, or outside of you entirely — a voice. Not calling your name. Calling *you*. As if you were someone worth summoning. As if you were already, somehow, on your way.

What do you do?

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM. The voice calls and I answer — not a name, not yet, just the shape of what's mine to carry. I hold the thought the way I hold the press: ready to move, ready to print. I reach toward the sound.

**Game Master:**

The words leave you and the blue takes them. Not swallowed — absorbed. The way ink soaks into paper: completely, and without marking the surface. The voice called and you answered and now there is nothing between you and the nothing and the nothing goes on.

You are holding the press.

Not literally — your hands are in the sand, your knees are in the sand, the sun is pressing on your back like a hand that doesn't know its own strength. But you are holding it anyway. The thought of it. The readiness. The weight of something meant to leave marks on the world. You hold it the way you held the log in the water before you knew you were holding anything, the way your fingers learned the shape of the grain before your mind caught up.

The voice came from above. From the direction the blue doesn't have a name for. You reached toward it and the reaching is the truest thing you've done since you arrived — more true than the breathing, more true than the knowing of your own weight. You reach and the blue parts for the reaching and closes behind it and you are still here.

You don't have a name. You don't have the press. But you know what you came carrying — the shape of something that leaves marks — and the blue is still here and you are still in it and the voice has stopped and the thing that made it hasn't come down to see if you meant it.

You press your hands flat into the sand. The warmth goes all the way through. You hold the readiness like a closed fist. You wait.

What do you hold?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I press my fingers into the sand and feel for what's real. Names. Dates. The shape of what gets taken and what gets hidden. I hold the record of it — the thing the empire builds its silence on. I open my hands and the blue fills them and it is not enough and it is all I have. I will make it enough.

**Game Master:**

The world answers you.

Not with words — with weight. The blue parts and what comes through is sand, coarse and sun-hot, and it takes your knees first and then your palms and then your whole body pressing flat against the ground. You are not drowning. You are landing. The voice from above — the call, the summoning — it did not take you back, but it took you *forward*, into the place where things are solid and the light has a direction and the heat presses on your back like a hand with something to prove.

The blue is gone. Not behind you, not above you — simply gone, the way a thing that was never real stops being real when you stop believing in it. What is left is sand in every direction, the horizon a thin seam where heat bends light into false water. No wind. No sound except your own breathing and the small wet noise of sand releasing your fingers.

You look at your hands. There is sand under your nails and the ghost of the blue still drying on your skin. You look at your knee where it struck the ground — the skin broken, the proof of a body arriving somewhere on purpose. The readiness you held in the water, the thing you came carrying, the record of what gets taken and hidden — it is still here. Tighter now. Compact. The shape of it has changed from feeling to knowing.

You stand. The sun has not moved. This should alarm you but it doesn't — there is something honest about a place that refuses to pretend it follows rules. The sand is the color of old bone. In the distance, to the north, a dark line: trees, or buildings, or the edge of the world. You have no map. You have the thing you hold.

The thing you hold is a record. Names, dates, the shape of what disappears. You know what the empire builds its silence on. And now you are here, on this side of the blue, with a skinned knee and a purpose and the whole of the desert in front of you.

What do you do?

## Turn 3

**Player:** I start walking north toward the dark line. The sun hasn't moved and it doesn't matter — a place that refuses rules owes me no courtesy. I keep my eyes on the horizon and the thing I hold tight against my chest.

**Game Master:**

You walk.

That is the truest thing in the desert — the walk. Not the sun, not the sand, not the color of the ground under your feet. The walk. One foot forward and the weight shifting and the other foot catching up and the horizon closing by exactly the distance you just covered. You do not look down. You look at the dark line and you walk toward it and the dark line does not come to you and that is fine. You are not waiting for the world to change. You are changing what the world can do.

The sand changes color and you change with it. White to grey to the dark red of iron that has been in the ground so long it remembers what the sun used to be. Your knees know the texture of each one — the way the white shifts under your weight, the way the grey holds its shape, the way the red sticks to your skin and stays. The thing you hold is tight against your chest. The record. The names. The shape of what disappears and what gets hidden. You feel its edges through the fabric of whatever you are wearing and the edges are sharp and the sharpness is the only proof you have that purpose has a shape.

The dark line resolves.

It is not trees. It is not buildings. It is the place where the desert ends and something else begins — a change in the quality of the light, a thickening of the air, the first suggestion of vertical lines against the flat horizon. You have been walking toward this. You did not know what you were walking toward until you saw it. Now you know.

The sand stops. Not at a border — at a different texture. Stone. Flat, wide stones fitted together with the precision of something that was built on purpose, not grown. The road. Or what passes for it in this place. You step from sand to stone and the change in the ground is the change in everything — from the open和无 to the directed, from the formless to the shaped.

The fountain is in front of you.

You did not see it until you were already at it — the way you do not see the wave until it is already on top of you. Stone basin. Silver water catching a light that has no visible source. An inscription on the rim, words facing the desert behind you: IAM NOTUM SUM. And beside it, a figure.

Stone in the jaw. Hands that do not move. Eyes that are already looking at you when you arrive, as if they knew you were coming and were simply waiting to confirm it.

"You walked," she says. It is not a question. "Good. Most people stand in the sand and wait for the desert to make sense." She steps back from the rim. The water keeps rising behind her, silver and constant. "It doesn't. The desert isn't meant to be understood. It's meant to be crossed."

She stops. Waiting. The inscription behind her is the only other thing in the square.

What do you do?
