The blue has no end. You drift in it — formless, weightless, neither rising nor sinking. Somewhere above there is a surface you cannot see, and somewhere ahead a truth you have not yet named. The only thing taking shape is the thing you cling to: a single concept, faint as ink in water, holding you here when everything else would let you dissolve. You are not alone in this — every drifter is alone in this — and you cannot reach them and they cannot reach you. You are not yet a thing that can be read. What do you hold?

## Turn 1

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM — Quill. I hold the press and the ink and the names that don't belong to the empire. The blue can dissolve everything else; these stay. The wheel turns and I go with it, carrying what I've always carried.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen.

The fountain is gone. The cobblestones are gone. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in.

What do you do?
