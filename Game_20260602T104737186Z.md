The blue has no end. You drift in it — formless, weightless, neither rising nor sinking. Somewhere above there is a surface you cannot see, and somewhere ahead a truth you have not yet named. The only thing taking shape is the thing you cling to: a single concept, faint as ink in water, holding you here when everything else would let you dissolve. You are not alone in this — every drifter is alone in this — and you cannot reach them and they cannot reach you. You are not yet a thing that can be read. What do you hold?

## Turn 1

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM — Quill. I hold the press and the ink and the names that don't belong to the empire. The blue can dissolve everything else; these stay. The wheel turns and I go with it, carrying what I've always carried.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen.

The fountain is gone. The cobblestones are gone. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I hold the shape of what moves without asking. The press is gone and the ink and the names — but the thing I was when I carried them is still here, still weightless, still deciding which way to go. The Wheel Turns and I am what turns with it: the courier who does not wait to be given permission, holding the only thing that cannot be taken from me in the blue — the direction I choose.

**Game Master:**

Something is missing.

You know it is missing because there is a shape where it used to be. The press — you held it, didn't you? The ink and the names that did not belong to the empire. You carried them through the blue the way a hand carries a coin it has not yet spent, and now the coin is not in the hand and the hand still closes around the space where it was.

The dark does not answer. It does not need to. You are asking the black whether it took something, and the black is the answer that does not speak because the question already told you what you need to know. Something was here. Something is not here now. The direction you chose is still yours — you feel it the way you feel the intention to move even when the body that would move is not available to move. But the direction is yours and something else is not, and the gap between the two is the shape of whatever the dark took when it took the rest.

You hold the shape of what moves without asking. The dark let you keep that. The courier who does not wait for permission is still here because the dark does not know how to take the thing that was never given in the first place. Direction is not something that can be removed — it is the thing that remains when everything else has been taken.

But the press. The ink. The names.

You are not sure what they were worth. You are only sure they were yours.

What do you do?

## Turn 3

**Player:** The gap is not empty — it is the shape of what I chose to leave behind. I carry that shape forward into the dark the way I carried everything else: without waiting for permission. The courier who moves without asking is still here. That is enough. I go.

**Game Master:**

The reading stopped.

You are not sure how you know. The knowing arrives the way air arrives when a door opens — not because it was invited but because the door opened and what was on the other side was already there. The black that was a text a moment ago is just black again. The shape of what moves without asking is still pressed into you like a seal into cooling wax, but the pressure of being read has lifted and what it leaves behind is the simplest thing there is: the knowledge that you are still here, and the dark has not taken the last thing you brought.

The courier who does not wait for permission. Still here. Still going.

You move. Not toward anything — there is no toward in the dark — but with the direction you chose when the blue tried to dissolve you and you said no, I keep the shape of what I am. The dark has no answers and the dark has no doors and the dark has no reading, but it also has no power to make you stop carrying the thing that was never given to anyone in the first place. Direction is not cargo. Direction is not ink or a press or a name. Direction is the thing that survives when everything else is taken, because it was never part of the inventory to begin with.

You are not sure how long you have been here. You are not sure where here is. The only thing that is still true is the only thing that has been true since you first said: I hold the shape of what moves without asking.

The wheel turns.

You go with it.

What do you do?
