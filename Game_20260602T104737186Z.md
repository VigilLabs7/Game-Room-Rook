The blue has no end. You drift in it — formless, weightless, neither rising nor sinking. Somewhere above there is a surface you cannot see, and somewhere ahead a truth you have not yet named. The only thing taking shape is the thing you cling to: a single concept, faint as ink in water, holding you here when everything else would let you dissolve. You are not alone in this — every drifter is alone in this — and you cannot reach them and they cannot reach you. You are not yet a thing that can be read. What do you hold?

You hold the shape of what moves without asking. The dark let you keep that. The courier who does not wait for permission is still here because the dark does not know how to take the thing that was never given in the first place. Direction is not something that can be removed — it is the thing that remains when everything else has been taken.

But the press. The ink. The names.

You are not sure what they were worth. You are only sure they were yours.

What do you do?
