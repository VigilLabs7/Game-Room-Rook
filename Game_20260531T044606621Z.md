The blue goes on. You don't know how long you've been drifting — there's no way to measure it here, no surface above, no floor below. Just blue in every direction, and the faint sense that you're holding onto something you can't quite name yet. Then: a voice, from somewhere outside the blue. *Come up.* The words land somewhere inside you and the blue around you thins. Something you're gripping — some truth you've been carrying without knowing it — catches the call. It grows branches. It pulls you upward. The blue parts.

You break through.

Sand. Sun. A body you didn't have a moment ago, solid now, real — a little scraped, a little stunned, taking its first breath of hot desert air. You're on your knees in white sand, and the world is loud with silence and wind.

A name would help. An idea of who you are — what you came here carrying — would help more. But those come later. Right now there's just the sand, and the heat, and the faint sense that you came here for a reason.

What do you do?
