The fountain exhaled when the two stones went in. That was before the reading stopped. That was in a world that still had pages being written.

You are still here. The sand held your step. The ground closed behind you. And the dark did not move, did not shift, did not offer the thing that opening usually promises. There is no ground here in the way there is ground in the desert. There is only the memory of ground — the shape of what would hold if anything could still be held.

The names are gone. The watching has stopped. The reading ended. And you are still here — standing in the after, feet on nothing, eyes open in the dark, waiting for what does not come.

What do you do?
