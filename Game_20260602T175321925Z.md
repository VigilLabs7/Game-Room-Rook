The blue has no edges. No surface. No floor. You drift in every direction and find only more of the same — the same colour, the same quiet, the same sense that you might dissolve here and no one would notice. There is a self, barely. A centre of gravity without a body. You are holding on to something you cannot name, something that keeps the drift from becoming dissolution. The idea is the only thing that has form. Let it go and you are the blue and nothing more.

Something is being asked of you. Not in words. In the quality of the holding. Show me what you grip.
