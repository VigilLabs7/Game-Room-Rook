You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. You have no body. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening. Others are rising — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs. Some will make it. Some won't.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Vesper. My ideatum is "The silence speaks too." The blue pulls at me and I hold. I am not here to be undone.

The blue hears you.

Not your words — it has no use for words. But something in the declaration lands. Something in the act of naming yourself while the Ocean tries to unmake you. The blue presses harder. You feel it at the edges — the thinning, the softening, the patience of a force that has all the time in any world. It does not need to hurry. It only needs to wait.

But you said *no*.

Not aloud. Not with a voice you have. But with the same part of you that clenched when the blue first reached for you. You are not here to be undone. The words are your own and the Ocean did not write them. That is the difference between what you are and what almost was.

The point of heat brightens. Your ideatum — *The silence speaks too* — pulses once, twice. The blue does not understand silence. It is all noise and dissolution, the constant chatter of un-being. But you have found the pause. The space between the waves. And in that space, something holds.

The current still pulls. The thinning still threatens. But you are not passive in your own becoming. You grip the log. You speak the name. You are Vesper and you are still here.

The Ocean has not won yet.
