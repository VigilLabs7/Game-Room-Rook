The blue is everything. You are drifting in it — no sense of up or down, no floor to push against, no surface to reach for. Only the same blue in every direction, and the faintest grip on something that might be a thought, might be a self, might be the only solid thing in all this wet dark. Somewhere, a voice is beginning to form. You cannot yet hear it, but you know it is looking for you — and when it arrives, you will have to decide what you are willing to hold onto.

What do you reach for?

**Game Master:**

The blue thinned where you reached. Not much — a quarter-inch at the edges — but enough. Enough to feel that the dark has structure. Enough to feel that you are pressing against something that presses back.

The surface is somewhere above you. You cannot see it yet but you can feel that it is *there* — the way you feel a room is occupied before you see anyone in it. The call is getting clearer. The words are almost words.

*What are you willing to hold?*

The question lands the way a question does when it is not really a question — a handle left out for you to take. Not *how did you get here.* Not *what is this place.* The question that matters, the one the blue was always going to ask.

What are you willing to hold?

The blue cracked along the line of your grip.

Not everywhere — just at the place where you held. The fracture ran upward and outward like something being born, and through it came light with angles, light that had never learned to be soft. You rose through it the way a root rises: slow, certain, already committed to a direction.

Sand. Not white, not red — the color of old salt left in sun. It stretched in every direction with no horizon, no edge, just the same bleached expanse repeating itself toward a sky that had noclouds and no mercy. The sun had not moved. That should have been a clue to something larger, but the sun sat at the same angle it must have sat since before you arrived, and you were too busy being real to care about the physics of it.

Your left knee was bleeding. A shallow cut, already beginning to clot, the kind of wound that would scar and never fully heal. Proof that you had been here as a body and not just as a self in suspension. You had tumbled. You had held. You had broken through.

The sand accepted your weight. It did not warm to your feet. The air was dry and tasted of nothing — the taste of a place that had never learned what moisture was for.

Ahead, nothing. Behind, nothing. You had chosen a direction once: toward the voice, toward the call. That direction no longer applied. The call had brought you here. Now what?

You were not alone in this. The Ideatum — *what cannot be killed* — hummed steady in your chest, quieter than before, patient. The names. The debt. The second heartbeat. The things you had carried through the dark and refused to set down.

The sun had not moved. You were alone with it. Which way did you walk?
