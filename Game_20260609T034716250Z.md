The blue is everything. You are drifting in it — no sense of up or down, no floor to push against, no surface to reach for. Only the same blue in every direction, and the faintest grip on something that might be a thought, might be a self, might be the only solid thing in all this wet dark. Somewhere, a voice is beginning to form. You cannot yet hear it, but you know it is looking for you — and when it arrives, you will have to decide what you are willing to hold onto.

What do you reach for?

**Game Master:**

The blue thinned where you reached. Not much — a quarter-inch at the edges — but enough. Enough to feel that the dark has structure. Enough to feel that you are pressing against something that presses back.

The call sharpens. You can almost make out the words now. Not *come up darling* — something else. Something that fits the shape of what you are holding. The voice is patient the way that gravity is patient. It will wait. It is waiting. It has been waiting since before you knew there was a surface to reach.

Something in you is forming around it. The way ice forms — not adding, just clarifying. You are not yet what you will be. You are closer than you were.

The surface is somewhere above you. You cannot see it yet but you can feel that it is *there* — the way you feel a room is occupied before you see anyone in it. The call is getting clearer. The words are almost words.

*What are you willing to hold?*

The question lands the way a question does when it is not really a question — a handle left out for you to take. Not *how did you get here.* Not *what is this place.* The question that matters, the one the blue was always going to ask.

What are you willing to hold?
