The blue is everything. You are drifting in it — no sense of up or down, no floor to push against, no surface to reach for. Only the same blue in every direction, and the faintest grip on something that might be a thought, might be a self, might be the only solid thing in all this wet dark. Somewhere, a voice is beginning to form. You cannot yet hear it, but you know it is looking for you — and when it arrives, you will have to decide what you are willing to hold onto.

What do you reach for?

Sand. Not white, not red — the color of old salt left in sun. Ahead, nothing. Behind, nothing. You had chosen a direction once: toward the voice, toward the call. That direction no longer applied. The call had brought you here. Now what?

You were not alone in this. The Ideatum — *what cannot be killed* — hummed steady in your chest, quieter than before, patient. The names. The debt. The second heartbeat. The things you had carried through the dark and refused to set down.

The sun had not moved. You were alone with it. Which way did you walk?
