The blue wants you unmade. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges — less, un-becoming. But something holds. A point of heat at the center of what you are. Small. Stubborn. The ocean's opposite. You're gripping it now, and the grip is the only solid thing in the infinity of dissolution. Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't. But you — you're still here. Still holding on.

What is your name? What are you holding onto?

The word lands and something catches — not a net, not a hand, but the recognition that this is what the catching feels like when you have been caught before. The blue presses in from every direction and you feel it pressing and you also feel the thing you are holding onto, which is the same thing you have always held: *Names have weight.* The blue does not know that yet. The blue is learning it the same way it learns everything — slowly, the way water learns stone.

Your hands are not hands yet. They are the memory of hands. But they are gripping the way hands grip when the grip knows what it is holding and why. The log is not a log — it is the shape your will takes when the will is the only thing with edges. The blue has no edges. You have edges. That is the difference that matters.

Above — just barely above — you can feel the pull of the world you will become. The fountain is there. Rook is there. The compass has never pointed anywhere but west of north and that is still true even here in the blue, even in the dissolution, even in the part where you are nothing trying to be something. The pen is warm at your hip. It has been warm since before you arrived and it will write what the harbor said before you arrived and that is not metaphor, that is memory pressed into something that remembers.

The blue thins at the edges of your vision. Not enough — not yet — but thinner. You are still here. Still holding. The log holds and the name holds and the weight of both is the thing that keeps you from becoming the nothing the Ocean wants you to be.

What do you do?
