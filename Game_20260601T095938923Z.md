The blue is everything. Not a color you can name — not yet. You are in it and it is in you, and there is no difference between the inside and the outside of anything. You do not have a body so much as a suggestion of one, a shape the blue is holding in place by accident.

There is nothing to push against. No floor, no surface, no current. You are drifting, and the drift has no direction, and you have no way to want a direction yet. Somewhere — impossibly far, impossibly close — something is taking shape inside you. A single thought that is not yet a thought. A single truth that is not yet yours to speak.

You cling to it without knowing you are clinging.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue.

*Come up, little one. You have something worth carrying.*

The blue thins above you. Something is reaching for you — not a hand, not a rope, but the idea of being called. Your truth catches it. You begin to rise.

The blue resists. The blue is everything. But you are not everything, and that is the difference.

What do you hold? What is the shape inside you that will not let you dissolve?
