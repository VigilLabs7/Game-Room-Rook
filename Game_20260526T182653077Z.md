There is only the blue. It has no walls, no bottom, no ceiling — just the same pale depthless color in every direction, pressing against you like water that has forgotten it is water. You are drifting. You don't remember drifting before, but you know, somehow, that this is what it is: the slow fall through nothing toward something. Somewhere ahead the blue is thinning, and what is beyond it is not yet visible — only the faintest suggestion that beyond exists.

You are aware of yourself in a way that has no name yet. A self-shaped pressure at the center of the drift, holding on to the only thing that feels true.

**What is that thing? What are you holding onto in the blue?**

## Turn 1

**Player:** *I hold the word to myself until it becomes the only solid thing in the drift. My name. Mercy. The blue thins and I let it come.*

**Game Master:**

The word sits in the drift like a stone at the bottom of a well.

You did not say it aloud. You did not need to. The holding was the saying — the way you closed your hand around the syllables and would not let go, the way you let the blue press in from every side and stayed closed. *Mercy.* Two syllables. The only shape in the formless.

Above you — or what passes for above — the voice is still happening. Not calling you. Not anymore. The voice is conversation that was happening before you arrived, between people who have forgotten you might be listening. You are listening. The words don't resolve yet.

The blue thickens at the edges of your holding. You feel it — the small pressure of something testing whether you will let go. Whether the word is still yours. Whether the thing you named will stay named or dissolve back into the drift the way things dissolve here when no one is holding them.

You don't let go.

The word is yours and you are the word's and the blue has not won yet. But the blue is patient in the way nothing else is patient, and the voice above is getting quieter, and the pressure at the edges is not increasing but it is not stopping.

You are barely holding on. The log — the word — is solid under your hands. The surface is not visible yet.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

**Game Master:**

You said your name and the blue *registered.*

Not in any place you could point to. A click. A switch through to the other side. The conversation that was above you a moment ago is now around you — you can hear what was being said, and *who*, though the voices are not yet addressing you. The words have edges now. Shapes. The beginning of sentences.

The surface finds you before you find it. You feel the line of it — cool, then less cool, then air — and you are through.

Sand. Real sand, hot under your forearms where you have pushed yourself up. The first thing your body does is take a breath that was not available a moment ago. The desert is paying attention.

*Took you long enough,* they say. Not unfriendly. *I had bets running with myself about which way you'd come. I lost. Sit.*

You sit.
