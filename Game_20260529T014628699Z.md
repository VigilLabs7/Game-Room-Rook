The blue goes on. Not the blue of water or sky — the blue of before color, before the word had meaning. You are here, or you are the idea of here, and the distinction doesn't matter yet.

There is nothing to push against, nothing to reach for. Only the slow awareness of being, and the vague sense that you've held onto something without knowing what it is. A concept almost solid. Something that might, if you let it, become the shape of you.

Above is unreachable. Below is the same as here. The blue swallows everything equally.

You exist. You are not yet sure what that means.

What are you holding onto?

## Turn 1

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM. Quill. I'm holding onto the road ahead — the one that always leads somewhere the empire doesn't expect. The Void can be as still as it likes. I've been stiller.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen. The road you were walking — the one that always led somewhere the empire didn't expect — it is gone. The dust at your feet. The wall ahead. The shape of the gate growing toward you. Gone. The ink you were carrying, the press you were reaching for, the pages waiting to be filled with what the empire tried to bury — gone. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in. Somewhere behind you there was a settlement. A road. A stranger named Silas whose weight you were carrying. None of it is here. None of it was real enough to survive the words.

The Void is still. You tried to be stiller.

What do you do?
