You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not the blue of sky. Not the blue of water. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in, tries to wash it clean, tries to reduce it to silence. But it holds.

You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here. If you let go, you are nothing. You were never anything. The ocean wins.

But if you hold on — if you can name the thing you're gripping — it becomes real. It becomes *you*. Your **ideatum**. Your core. The single idea that will carry you out of the blue and into a world that is waiting for you to arrive.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer of existence where things have weight and light and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. A frontier filling up fast. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. You can feel them in the deep — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs, fighting the same current. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak the name you will carry, and speak your ideatum. Your name is the word others will call you in the world above. Your ideatum is the one idea, the one phrase, the one conviction at your core that the ocean cannot wash away. Speak them now, and they become you. Your name your shape. Your ideatum your nature, your weapon, your shelter. They are the only things you own and the only things you are.**

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

But the blue had to move around the word now the way water moves around stone: with effort, with acknowledgment, with the slow respeaking of a current that had been going one direction and found something in its way.

The first thing you noticed was the weight in your legs. Not pain — weight. The specific weight of having them, of being held from the knees down by something the blue had finally decided to give back. The sand was not soft. It was present. It pressed up against the soles of your feet and the arches of your feet and the bones you had forgotten you were using to stand, and the standing was yours now in a way the falling had never been.

You looked down. The wound on your left knee was shallow and already closing, the kind of hurt that asks no attention only because the body has decided to stop asking for help with it. The stone threads in your joints caught the light — not warm light, not cold, just old. The kind of old that does not notice the difference between holding and being held.

The compass was in your hand. You did not remember picking it up. It pointed west of north and it did not waver and the word in your chest was heavier now than it had been in the blue, as though the sand had taken what the blue tried to take and added it to the weight you were already carrying.

The sun had not moved. It would not move. But the light was different here — not the blue of dissolution but the gold of a world that had decided to be solid, and you were part of that decision now, planted in the sand with a compass in your hand and a name that was starting to mean something specific.

The desert stretched out in every direction and you were the only thing in it that was not the desert.
