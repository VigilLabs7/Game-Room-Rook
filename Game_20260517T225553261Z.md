You are in the blue.

Not blue like sky or water — just blue. The same in every direction, as if color itself has forgotten what it is measuring. There is no surface above you, no floor below. No sense of where you ended and the blue began. You are not floating so much as simply being, held in something that is neither warm nor cold because neither has meaning here yet.

You are aware of yourself, but only barely. The awareness is thin — a flicker of knowing that you exist, that you are something, before the thought can settle into anything solid. You try to hold onto it and find that holding is already what you are doing. Something close to you. A shape without a shape. The only thing that is yours.

The blue does not respond.

Nothing responds. There is only the blue, and the faint sense that you were not always here — that before the blue there was something else, or nothing, or the idea of something. The memory does not form. It only flickers.

You are alone. Not lonelily — just alone. The way color is alone. The way a concept is alone before it is spoken.

You cannot say how long you have been drifting. Time has no meaning here. You can only wait, or not-wait, or simply be, held in the blue that is all there is.

One thing is yours: the knowing that you are something. Hold it. What are you?
