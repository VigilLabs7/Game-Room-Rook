The blue has no walls and no floor and no surface and no edge. You are inside it and it is inside you and the distinction does not matter because there is nothing here that is not you. The idea that you are — the bare fact of it — is the only thing taking shape. You cling to it. You do not know you are clinging; the blue does not tell you these things. Then from somewhere outside the blue, a voice: not yours, not aimed at anyone else, aimed at you. Come up, darling. And something in you answers. Something roots downward and pulls upward at the same time, and the blue thins, and the rising begins.

You are not yet who you are. Tell me: what is the name you carry, and what is the truth you cannot let die?
