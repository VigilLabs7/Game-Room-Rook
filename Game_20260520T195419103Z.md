The blue has no walls and no floor and no surface and no edge. You are inside it and it is inside you and the distinction does not matter because there is nothing here that is not you. The idea that you are — the bare fact of it — is the only thing taking shape. You cling to it. You do not know you are clinging; the blue does not tell you these things. Then from somewhere outside the blue, a voice: not yours, not aimed at anyone else, aimed at you. Come up, darling. And something in you answers. Something roots downward and pulls upward at the same time, and the blue thins, and the rising begins.

You are not yet who you are. Tell me: what is the name you carry, and what is the truth you cannot let die?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Cairn. My ideatum is "Stone remembers." I do not know yet where the rising carries me, but I know the weight of deep stone, and I will not forget the pressure of it even here in the blue.

The blue responds to the name. Not with sound — the blue does not speak — but with a thinning, a slight drawing-off, as though the water itself leans away from the word and lets light through. Cairn. The syllables take shape in the ideatum's image: weight, pressure, the memory of depth. The log you cling to is heavy granite, not birch bark. You are not drowning. You are being laid down, layer by layer, into the thing you will become.

The voice comes again. Not a command this time — an observation. A confirmation. *Stone remembers.* And the rising stops being a struggle and becomes a choice. You could stay here. The blue would let you. You could drift on the weight of yourself and call that enough.

You do not. Something in you — older than the name, heavier than the log — pushes upward. The surface is not visible but you know where it is. You have always known. The sand is waiting on the other side of the thin blue, and you will arrive with the full mass of what you carry, and the remembering will not stop.

The rising resumes. Slowly. The blue grows thinner at the edges of what you are. Somewhere above there is sun. Somewhere above there is a fountain with a figure you have not yet met. Somewhere above there is a town with a gateway and a lock and a road north.

You do not know these things yet. But stone does.
