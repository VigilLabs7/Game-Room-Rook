The blue is everywhere. Not a color — a condition. You drift in it without a sense of up or down, no floor to push off, no surface reaching for you. There is only the awareness that you are, suspended in submergent blue, and in the blur around you — nothing. No edges. No other presences. The solitude is total.

But something is catching. A shape in the blue, barely. A single concept taking the only form that can exist here — the thing you will not let go of, the truth that has not yet been given a voice. It clings to you. You cling back.

Something is reaching. Not from below. Not from beside. From above — a voice cutting through the blue like light through water. You cannot make out the words yet, but the concept at your center responds to it. The log grows roots. The blue thins.

**What do you do?**
