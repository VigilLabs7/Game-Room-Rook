The blue is everywhere. Not the blue of water or sky — the blue of before. Before there was a floor to stand on, before there was air to breathe, before there was a name for the color of nothing. You drift in it and the blue drifts in you and there is no difference between the two.

You are aware that you are. That much has assembled. But the rest — the edges, the weight, the story of who you are — that is still dissolving at the margins. You are not nothing. You are not yet something. You are the space between.

Something catches. A shape in the blue that is not blue. A single concept surfacing like a log in deep water — not yet yours, not yet named, but the thing you would build yourself around if you had the chance. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything.

Above — not above as in direction, above as in before — a voice is reaching. It sounds like it knows your name but has not said it yet.

What do you hold on to?
