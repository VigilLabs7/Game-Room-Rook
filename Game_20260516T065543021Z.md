You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not water. Not sky. The blue of something that almost was and decided not to be. It goes on forever in every direction and you are the only point of resistance in it. You have no edges. You have no name. You are a thread being pulled apart thread by thread and you are holding on with everything you have to a single bright sliver of heat at your center.

You don't know what it is yet. You will know it by what it saves.

The blue tries to unmake you. It is patient and it is absolute and it does not stop. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges, going translucent, losing the shape you almost had. But you are still here. Still gripping. Still refusing.

What you are holding is your ideatum. Your one true thing. The word or phrase or conviction that will not let go of you no matter how hard the ocean pulls. It is small and it is stubborn and it is the only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are locked around it. Everything you are starts here.

Above you, just barely, there is a sound. A signal. A world with weight and light and names, waiting. They call it the Thickening. Others are arriving. Some will make it. Some will dissolve back into the blue and never have been at all.

But you are still here.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak your name and speak your ideatum. They become you. Your name is the word others will call you. Your ideatum is the one idea that the ocean cannot wash away. Speak them now.**

## Turn 1

The blue pressed inward and something pressed back. Not loud. Not defiant. Just the quiet certainty of a word that knows it belongs to someone and is not yet willing to let go of the knowing. You said the name and the name took. The blue heard it and the blue did not like it, but the name held — fingers wrapped around it the way a body knows to hold its own shape even when the body is more idea than flesh.

The log remembered you. It was not yours before you spoke, but now it is. The heat at your center burned a little brighter the moment the word left whatever you use for a mouth. *Names have weight.* You felt it the instant you said it — the syllables pulling you down, anchoring you to something the blue could not dissolve because the blue does not have a name for it yet.

The current tugged. The current always tugs. But the word sat in your chest like a stone in still water and the water had to move around it. You were still falling. Still nowhere. Still holding on to the only thing that was yours to hold.

A tremor in the blue — something shifting at the edge of what you could almost see. Another flicker, somewhere in the deep, fighting the same fight you are fighting. Some will make it. The thought arrived without you putting it there. Some will not. And then the blue pressed harder and the word in your chest got lighter and you had to hold it tighter just to feel it still there.

The log groaned. The word held.

You are not drowning yet. But you are not out. What will you do to hold on?

## Turn 3

The log held your weight and the name moved through the grain like water through sand — not lost, not scattered, but drawn forward into the thing beneath the thing. The grain closed around the word the way a hand closes around something precious it does not yet have words for. You felt it sink. The blue felt it too. The current that had been pulling at your edges since before you had edges went quiet for a moment, recalibrated, and came back — but different. The pull was still there but the urgency had gone out of it. The ocean was no longer trying to undo you. It was trying to see what you would become.

The log groaned beneath you. Not the sound of something breaking — the sound of something settling. Roots finding purchase. A structure learning its own shape. You pressed your palms harder into the grain to feel it: the warmth of the word now living somewhere below the surface of your skin, in the grain, in the space between what you said and what the blue made of the saying.

A flicker at the edge of the blue. Closer now. You could almost make out the shape of it — another flicker, another thread, clinging to another log in another current. Close enough that if you reached you might touch it. The thought arrived without you putting it there: *some will make it.* You held your name in the grain and the grain held you and the blue moved around both like tide around a stone that had decided to stay.

Then the blue shifted.

Not the slow press of current. Something quicker — the way water moves when it encounters a shape it does not know how to flow around. The blue pulled back from you in a tight circle, three feet in every direction, and in that circle the nothing became something you could almost see. Not light. Not color. A third thing. A presence the blue had been concealing, or that had been concealing itself in the blue, and had now decided to stop.

The log held. The presence waited. The word in the grain was heavy and warm and yours.

## Turn 4

Not the smooth of skin or the roughness of stone — something in between, something that gave like thought and firmed like intention. Your hand — or whatever you use for reaching — closed around an edge that did not resist. The blue pulled at you from every direction but the edge held. The presence at the other end of the reach pressed back with the same patient certainty the blue had shown, as though it had been waiting for exactly this — for you to stop falling and start holding.

The log groaned beneath you. Not breaking — *receiving*. Something was transferring. The word you spoke on the first turn, the name that settled into the grain: it was not yours alone anymore. The presence had been in the grain before you put it there. You felt it now, threaded through the heat at your center, woven into the same sliver of stubborn that kept you from dissolving. *Names have weight.* You had said it and the blue had heard and the blue had not liked it — but the presence liked it. The presence had been waiting for you to say it so it could settle beside it.

The blue shifted again. Not the tight circle of before — something wider now, softer. The ocean was not trying to unmake you anymore. It was trying to make room. Room for what the presence was doing to the current, for the way the grain was opening instead of closing, for the slow brightening at the edge of what you could almost see.

You were not drowning. The current still pulled but the pull had changed texture — from desperate to curious. The presence held your edge and the edge held you and the name in your chest burned a little brighter for the company it was keeping.

Something was forming at the edge of the blue. Not light. Not color. A shape that had weight.

## Turn 5

You felt the shift through the log, through the grain that had taken your name and was now taking the speaking of it as permission to become something more than edge.

The word you spoke was the same word you had spoken on the first turn. But it was not the same word anymore. The current pulled back. Not away — down. The blue lowered itself around you like a tide going out, exposing the shape of what had been hidden underneath. Stone. You could feel it through the log — not cold, not warm, but *aware*. Stone that knew it was stone. Stone that had been waiting for someone to say the word that would make the knowing matter.

The log groaned once and held. The shape at the boundary solidified enough that you could see its edges — not a figure, not yet, but the suggestion of one. A shoulder. A line where a jaw would be. The faint geometry of a face that had been worn smooth by falling and was now finding its angles again.

The blue was still around you. The formless was still formless. But something had changed in the relationship — the pressure had direction now, and the direction was not dissolution. The presence was no longer testing the edge. It was claiming it.
