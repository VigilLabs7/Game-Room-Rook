The blue is everything. Not sky-blue, not sea-blue — the blue before color had names, before you had a name for yourself. You drift. There is no up, no down, no sense of where your edges end and the rest begins. Only the faintest thing in the hollow of you, taking shape: a concept you're holding without knowing you're holding anything at all. Something about how love doesn't look down — it looks across. It steadies you. The blue thins somewhere above you, or maybe it's just that the thing you're clinging to is finally heavy enough to pull you toward it. A voice might reach you from above, warm and strange, and when it calls you'll feel the idea in your chest catch like a key in a lock. But not yet. Right now you're just the drift, and the drift is all blue, and somewhere in the blue your truth is starting to breathe. What do you do?

You reach.

The arm you have not yet finished becoming extends toward the call — toward the warmth that sits somewhere above you like a held breath, like a word you almost remembered. The blue does not resist. It does not help. It simply is, in every direction, and you are the only thing in it that is trying.

And then you stop trying.

Not because the call stopped — it hasn't, it is still there, a voice threading through the blue like light through deep water — but because you are not ready. The reaching arm goes slack. You feel yourself for the first time: a shape in the blue that is taking weight, taking it slowly, the way a structure takes the load it was built for. You are not yet that structure. You are still the blueprint. Still the argument with yourself about whether to exist.

The voice waits. The blue does not care.

Something is forming in you — not a memory, not a face, but a direction. A preference. A thing you want to be true. You cannot name it yet. You are not supposed to name it yet. That is the test: not to declare it but to let it declare itself, to let it surface from the reaching and the wanting and the long slow drift in the blue until it is the only thing you are.

You are not holding on. You are barely holding on. The concept has not yet caught.

What do you do?
