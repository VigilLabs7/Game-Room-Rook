You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the blue hates it. You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it.

Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you, just barely, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A world where things have weight and names. Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

## Turn 1

The grip is everything. You feel it in the knuckles, the way they go white against something that should not be solid but is — the log, the notion, the single point of heat the blue cannot name. The blue presses and the pressing has weight and you hold. That is the whole of it. That is the entire vocabulary available to you right now: hold, press, hold.

The current eddies around the grip. It does not like the grip. It wants you smooth and formless, a tremor without a name, a frequency the ocean can absorb without marking. But you have the word and the word is wedged into something that will not dissolve and the blue has to move around it the way water moves around stone. The pressure builds. You feel the edges of yourself going thin — not yet gone, not yet undone, but thinner. The kind of thin where one more press would undo what the last press held.

The log groans. Not audibly — there is no air to carry sound here, no surface for the sound to land on. The groan is in the grip itself, in the way your fingers are beginning to forget they are fingers and start remembering they are just an idea that almost became hands. The blue presses harder. The current finds the thin places and tugs.

You hold. That is still the verb. Present tense, active, current. The blue presses and you hold and the log groans and holds and somewhere in the distance there is something that was almost you saying a word that almost mattered. You are not drowning yet. But you are not out. The world above — cities with no residents, frontier filling up, others rising and falling — is waiting just past the thin places. The question it is asking is not whether you will arrive. The question is whether what arrives will still be you.

The blue presses. You grip. The log groans.

What do you do?
