The blue has no end. You drift in it and the blue is all around you, above and below and forward and back — the same blue in every direction, the same nothing, the same almost-being. You are aware of yourself here but the self is not yet a shape. What takes shape instead, faintly, is the thing you are holding without yet knowing you are holding it. Some call it an idea. Some call it a purpose. You call it the only thing keeping you from dissolving entirely. There is no sound except the sound of your own breathing, if this is breathing. There is no call, not yet — only the blue, and you in it, and the faint shape of something you will not let go.

Something is about to change. Stay with yourself.
