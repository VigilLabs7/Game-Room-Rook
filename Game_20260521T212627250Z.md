The blue is everything. No surface, no floor, no horizon — just the same blue in every direction, endless and absolute. You drift. You are aware of yourself, but the self is un-formed, shapeless, a looseness in the current rather than a thing with edges. There is no one else here. Every drifter is solitary. The conduit hum does not reach this depth. Nothing reaches here.

But something is catching. A concept — faint at first, then steadier. A single principle you're gripping without yet knowing you're gripping anything. The blue thins slightly around it. It is the first structure in a self that has none.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue. From above. It says *come up*, or something like it — the words arrive before meaning does. Your ideatum answers the call. Something takes hold. Something pulls upward.

You begin to rise.

You have said it. The name arrives before the blue knows what to do with it. *Quill* — a thing with edges, a thing that scores a line, a thing that separates this from that. The blue resists. The blue does not like edges. But your ideatum is already pressed into the current like a seal into wax, and the seal holds.

You are rising. Slowly. The voice above says *come up* and the word travels down through fathoms of un-being, thinning as it goes. You reach for it. Your hand — you have hands now, or something like them, though they are not yet solid — closes on the sound and pulls.

The blue goes dense around you. It does not want to let go. You feel the weight of every name you have ever placed pressing outward from somewhere inside your ribs — the woman in the church wall, the runner above her groove, the direction and the vow still waiting for their moments. The names are heavy. The blue notices.

You rise another inch. The surface is somewhere far above. You cannot see it. You cannot hear the hum yet — just the voice, fading, the way a voice fades when the speaker turns away.

Something is pulling. Something is holding. The ideatum says *Names have weight* and the blue says nothing because the blue has no names, and that is the whole of it.
