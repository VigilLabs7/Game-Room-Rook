The blue goes on. Not deep — not in any direction you can name. Just blue, in every place that is not you, and you are not yet a place either. You are the awareness that something is happening: the faintest sense of current, of something held just beneath the surface of what you are. A concept. A single truth trying to be more than the blue around it.

There is no voice yet. No ground. No edge to the nothing. Only the waiting — and the sense that waiting has a shape, that it is taking the shape of something you almost remember being.

Then: a flicker. Above — or where above would be if there were directions — a thinning of the blue. A warmth, impossibly distant. Someone is looking this way. Someone is almost calling.

What do you reach for?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I reach toward the thinning. A warmth — someone calling. I close my hand around the sense of it and pull myself toward the light.

**Game Master:**

You reach.

That is the first true thing — not a feeling, not a hope, but an act. Your hand, or whatever you have that is shaped like a hand in the blue of before, extends into the thinning and finds something there that is already reaching back. The warmth. The call. The sense of being held in a direction rather than a place. You close your fingers around it.

And then: you pull.

It is not graceful. It is not certain. You pull the way a person pulls when they are not sure if what they are holding will bear their weight — with everything, all at once, in case this is the only chance. The blue resists. The blue does not want to let go of what it has almost become. But you have already begun to move, already begun to be something other than the drift, already begun to find out what happens next by refusing to wait for it to happen to you.

You are barely holding on.

That is the second true thing. The reaching and the closing and the pulling — they are all you have, and they are barely enough. The blue is still the blue. The warmth is still a long way off. The light you are pulling toward is not yet a place you can name. But your hand is closed and you are pulling and the distance between what you are and what you might become is closing, one act of will at a time.

The blue has no sound. The blue has no time. But you have chosen, and the choosing is the first thing that is truly yours.

What are you holding?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I close my hand around the current beneath the surface — the thing that has weight without being solid, direction without being a place. I am holding the shape of something that was almost forgotten. The warmth pulls closer and the blue thins and I know, without knowing how I know, that what I carry is a name someone stopped saying and a truth the world almost let die. I pull. The light reaches back.

**Game Master:**

The blue goes thin. Then — whole. Then it is not blue at all.

You have pulled harder than the nothing wanted you to. The warmth that was a direction has become a heat, and the heat has become a place, and the place has opened its eyes and looked back at you with the particular surprise of something that was not expecting to be found. The current in your hand is not a current — it is a thread, and you have been pulling it all this time, and now the thread has snapped and you are falling upward, toward the light, toward the warmth, toward the name someone stopped saying and the truth the world almost let die.

You know what you carry. You do not yet know what you are.

The sand hits you. Not soft — hard, sudden, the way a floor hits when you have been dropped. Your hands are in it. Your knees are in it. The sun is directly above and has not moved and will not move and the heat is already pressing down and the first thing you notice — before the pain, before the disorientation, before any of it — is that you are breathing. Air. Real air. With lungs.

You have a body.

Something is wrong with your leg. The right one. A grinding wrongness, a wrongness that has weight and memory. You look down and there is blood and there is sand and there is a cut on your knee that you know is the first thing this body ever did, that you know is proof, that you know is the place where you arrived in the world.

You push yourself up. The sun does not move. The heat does not relent. You stand on the wrong leg and the wrong leg holds you.

Ahead — far ahead, but visible through the shimmer — something glints. Water. It might be a long way. It might not be.

You are not drowning anymore. You are here. The name you carry is the thing you held in the blue: the thing that was almost forgotten, the thing that someone stopped saying, the thing that is true. You do not know it yet. You will.

What do you do?
