The blue is a pressure without weight. A weight without gravity. It comes from everywhere and nowhere, pressing in on all sides at once, and you are in it the way a word is in a sentence before the sentence has been spoken — not yet meaningful, but taking shape from the pressure of everything around it.

You do not have a body here. Or you have the suggestion of a body — the memory of one, or the blueprint of one, the shape of where a body might be if a body were something you needed. What you have instead is the grip. The grip on the thing you are holding. The ideatum. The concept that is deciding what it will become by being held.

The blue grows more attentive around the grip. Something vast and patient is turning its mind toward you — not hostile, not kind, just curious in the way that something very large is curious about something very small. The grip tightens without you tightening it. The rope becomes real because you are real enough to hold it.

A voice. Not from below. From above — somewhere outside the blue, somewhere that is not here. It says something like: *come up, darling.* The words are not for you yet. They are the shape of a call that is finding what it is looking for. What do you hold onto? What is the name you carry?
