You drift in a place that has no name and no time — only the weight of what you might become, pressing against you like breath held too long. Shapes move past, half-formed, carrying the shadows of lives not yet chosen. Something vast is gathering just beyond the edge of you. You are not anyone yet. Soon, that will change.

## Turn 1

You step forward and the world resolves around you — not the nowhere you left, but a place with weight and edges. The air tastes of salt and something older, carries the hum of energy moving through conduits you cannot see. Lanterns sway on cables somewhere ahead, their light smeared by fog. A harbor is taking shape in the grey, and at its edge a form is waiting, neither fully there nor fully absent. You carry your name like something heavy. You haven't spoken it yet. The fog thickens as you approach.

## Turn 2

The barkeep sets down her glass. Lagota does not welcome, but tonight something new is here — Rook, two swords, a cracked monocle on a brass cord. Damian follows, paladin bearing the weight of a search with no visible end. Aethius is mist and tendril and not entirely here.

The pen in your pocket has already written your name on a clean page. You feel it warm against your thigh.

## Turn 3

The barkeep crosses to the far end of the bar without being asked and returns with something amber in a glass the color of old ale. She sets it down where Rook can reach but close enough that you can see the way her thumb bears down on the rim — not nervousness, something practiced. Rook accepts without comment. The monocle catches the firelight and for a moment the inscription on its frame resolves — not letters you can read, not any system you know. Damian takes the wall by the door. Aethius thins at the edges the way he does when the room has too many people in it. Your pen writes something against your thigh that you did not tell it to.

## Turn 4

The barkeep slides the glass toward the middle of the bar without looking at it. Rook takes a slow drink. The amber catches light from somewhere you can't identify — not a candle, not the street outside. A second lamp, or something the building itself exhales. Rook's monocle hangs still against his chest. Damian shifts his weight near the door. Aethius has gone quiet in the way that means he's listening to something in the walls. The pen in your pocket is warm, and you haven't told it to write anything.
