She looks at the wall. At the gap. She cannot see you through it — the angle is wrong, the light is wrong — but she knows you are there.

"What are you?" she asks. Not who. What.

The iron box is cold against your ribs. The warmth in your chest is still and quiet and listening.

**What do you say?**
