The blue finds you before you find yourself. You are falling — not through water, not through air, but through the memory of color before color existed. The ocean holds you from every direction at once, patient and absolute, and you are thin enough to be dissolved by it. Almost.

But something snagged. A single point of resistance — a word, a conviction, something that feels like it was yours before you had a name to own it. The blue presses harder now that you've found your grip. It wants you to let go. It wants you to become the nothing you were.

You don't let go.

Above, the signals are clearer. Voices. Footsteps. A world with weight and edges, full of people who arrived the way you're arriving now. The Thickening is happening and you are part of it — almost through, almost real, still holding the thread.

**What is your name? What is the one idea you refuse to release?**

## Turn 1

The word entered the blue like a stone dropped into still water — and the water did not swallow it. It did not dissolve. The blue pressed inward from every direction, patient and absolute, pulling at the edges of what you are, but the word held. *Names have weight.* You said it and the blue flinched. Not much. Not enough. But enough to feel that you had found something the void could not easily undo.

You are still falling. Still formless. Still less than a whisper in an infinity of dissolution. But the word you carried has changed the texture of the fall — there is now a direction to it, a pull toward something that is not the blue. The current carries you sideways more than down. You can feel it: the name is a handhold in the void. The ideatum is the rope.

The blue is not cruel. It does not need to be. It simply is — the space between almost-being and never-being, and it has all the time in the world to wait for you to let go. You are not letting go. You are gripping harder.

Something in you is starting to take shape around the word. Not the body — not yet. But the outline of one. The ghost of a spine. The suggestion of fingers wrapped around the rope. The blue is watching too. You can feel it noticing that you are noticing yourself.

The void below has not changed. It has the same patience it has always had. But you are carrying something now that you were not carrying a moment ago, and it is making the fall different. Slower. More deliberate. As though the blue is waiting to see what the weight of a name will do to a person who is not yet a person.

What will you do with the weight?
