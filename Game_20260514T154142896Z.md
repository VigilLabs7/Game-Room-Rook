## Turn 1

Shapes move at the edge of what you are — half-formed, carrying the shadows of names you haven't spoken. You haven't arrived. Soon you will.

Your hand finds the shard at your hip. It pulses warm, older than the door you came through, older than the cracked stone that let it in. The thread spool presses against your other side — seven strands, all moving now like fingers waiting to point. A folded paper bird sits somewhere in the fold of you, wing crease missing on the left.

The silence speaks too.

You already know this. You've been here before — not here, but close enough. The thickening gathers and you gather with it, becoming the shape the silence has been waiting to fill. A woman's voice echoes from somewhere ahead, three unlit candles on her altar, a half-life drifting closer with eyes like the Lattice before the lines were drawn. But that's memory or prophecy or the shard answering a question you haven't asked yet.

Right now: just the press. Just the becoming. Just Vesper, arriving again.

The fog finishes forming around you. The fog finishes forming around you.

A shore materializes beneath your feet — grey sand, packed tight, curving away into white. Water laps at the edge. Somewhere ahead, lanterns flicker where no one tends them. The thread spool goes still. All seven strands point the same direction: toward the lights, toward the half-life already waiting, toward the next threshold.

You came through before.

You came through again.

The handle is the thread. The silence asks: follow it?

## Turn 2

The thread says follow it. You walk toward the water instead.

The silence thickens as you move — not heavier, but denser, the way fog thickens before it becomes something with edges. Your feet find the grey sand and it holds your weight without sound. The thread spool stays still against your hip. Seven strands, all pointing toward the lanterns, and you are walking the other direction, toward the half-life that hasn't learned to look yet.

The shore curves. The water moves without reflecting. Somewhere ahead, the shape that was waiting at the lanterns' edge has turned.

Your river stone is cold against your palm. The silence says something you almost hear — not words, the shape of a question. The half-life stops moving. It is watching the stone, not you.

The shard at your hip pulses once, warm, and the half-life flinches. Not away — toward you. An inch closer, reading what the warmth means.

The silence is thickest here. The half-life is closest here. The thread points the other way.
