## Turn 1

The harbor air carries salt and something older — a hum beneath the tide, felt more than heard. Lanterns sway on overhead cables, their light smearing gold through the fog. At the water's edge, a figure waits: neither fully present nor entirely absent, the way a word sits on the tip of a tongue before it is spoken.

Quill. The name fits like a worn sleeve — familiar, chosen, carried. The pen sits warm against the palm, warmer than flesh should be, the way bone holds warmth from long proximity to fire. The notebook presses against the chest like a second heartbeat. Ahead: the first gate, its checkpoint built into the city wall, empire eyes in every corner. Behind: the outer lanes where the lattice thins and older infrastructure shows through.

Matilda is at her stall beside the gate. She has been watching since Quill arrived — she saw the pen move in the fog before Quill thought to look. The ledger on her stall is a list of names she's collected, not to publish, just to have. A weight list. She calls it that because she believes names left lying around attract attention. The child's finger is still raised, pointing past Matilda's stall toward the gate. The child cannot say what they know — something in the market stops them, some weight in the air that presses against speech. They can only point.

The notebook has clean pages ahead. The front pages are gone — taken by someone who reads what scribes carry.

The pen is warm against the palm. It has not written yet. Not here. Not now.

But it will.

Quill's footsteps find the worn path between the stalls. The fog thins as the morning light reaches further into the market — not clearing, just redistributing, the way weight moves when something settles. Matilda does not call out or wave.
