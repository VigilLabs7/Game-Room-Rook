You are not yet anyone. Around you, possibility stirs like heat over stone — half-seen, not yet solid. The weight of what you might become gathers without a shape, pressing inward from every direction at once. Something is about to catch you, and when it does, you will be made. The thickening is almost here.

## Turn 1

The stillness holds you a moment longer — and then the world moves.

The fog does not lift so much as step aside, one layer peeling back to reveal the next. Harborside air fills your lungs: salt, wet stone, something organic rotting beneath the surface. The notebook presses flat against your ribs where the pages are gone. The pen stays warm in your palm — has been warm since before you arrived, and you have stopped questioning why.

Three pilings stand ahead like a row of questions waiting to be asked. A cart beneath canvas, no driver. A figure between you and it — same grey coat you have seen before, same eyes that read stone like open text. The papers in the figure's hand catch the grey light. Columns of names. Most crossed out. The column below each name — empty, waiting.

"Language," the figure says, not looking up. "And name. The rest is filed and forgotten."

The knowing eyes lift to meet yours. They are waiting again. They have always been waiting — every game, every crossing, the same first question and the same weight of something behind it. But the pen in your hand is heavier than it has been before. Heavier, and not entirely yours.

Your mouth opens. A name comes out — your name, the one you chose or the one that chose you, it is difficult to say. The figure writes it in a column. Looks up. Waits.

The pen presses its angle into your palm. The fog has eyes. You can feel them on the back of your neck.

"Language," the figure repeats. "The weight comes next. What you carry — what carries you. Speak it plainly or the column stays empty and the gate stays shut."

The water moves against the pilings with the sound of something patient. The knowing eyes do not blink.
