You drift in the blue. Not water-blue, not sky-blue — the blue of something that has never been anything yet, the blue before color was sorted into names. There is no orientation in it, no up pulling at you, no floor below. You exist, which is the only miracle available here, but the existence has no shape you could draw. You are holding on to something. The only thing keeping you from dissolving into the unformed blue. A voice comes from beyond the blue, from above it, and it says something that makes the thing you're gripping feel suddenly like it could grow into a shape. The blue softens around the edges of what you are becoming.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold tighter. The voice speaks and the blue reshapes around what I'm becoming — but a thought surfaces: who is speaking? I open my mouth in the unformed dark and ask, quietly, *who are you?*

**Game Master:**

The question dissolves before it finishes leaving your mouth. The blue takes it the way the blue takes everything — completely, without residue. The voice that had spoken from above does not answer. The blue itself answers, reshaping around your doubt, around the question you asked, pressing harder into the edges of what you are becoming.

You feel yourself thin at the edges. The place where the question was — where the reaching-toward-something happened — the blue moves in to fill it. For a moment there is almost nothing there. Almost not-you.

You grip tighter. The memory of the voice, the shape it promised, the sense of something growing into a form instead of dissolving — you hold that. You hold it the way you held the first thing, when the blue was all there was and you were only the fact of existing.

The blue thins. The edges of you come back. You are still here.

The question cost you something. The blue took it and gave you back this: still holding, still unnamed, still becoming. The blue does not answer who is speaking. It only answers that something is, and that you are still reaching for it, and that the reaching is what keeps the edges of you where they are.

You hold on.

## Turn 2

**Player:** I do not ask again. The question cost me — I felt myself thin at the edges when the blue took it. Instead, I hold on and wait. I watch the place where the voice came from. The blue softens around the edges of what I am, and the voice pauses, as if waiting for me to find the question it will not take.

**Game Master:**

The blue notices that you are noticing it. That is enough to change what happens next.

You do not ask. The blue reshapes around your not-asking the way water reshapes around a stone that has stopped swimming — pressing in, finding the edges, doing the work the stone no longer does. You feel the thinning again, the soft pull at the margins of what you are. But you do not pull back from it. You hold where you are and let the blue come to you.

The voice pauses. For a long moment the blue holds its breath, and you hold yours, and the shape you have been becoming waits in the stillness between you. The voice has no face and no direction, but you can feel it working — as if it is building something just out of reach of your becoming, something that needs your asking to become real.

Then it comes. Not words. Not yet. A pressure in the blue itself, a thickening, a sense that something is trying to arrive at the edge of your held-and-waiting self. The blue goes momentarily thick as syrup, and in that thickness you feel something that might be a question shaped like a key, or a key shaped like a question — not given to you, but held in the blue, waiting for you to reach.

You feel the shape of it. You cannot quite touch it. The blue presses closer, the way a room presses closer when everyone has stopped speaking and is waiting for one person to say the thing that will change the room.

What do you do?

## Turn 3

**Player:** I reach. The shape meets my held-and-waiting hands and settles into them — the blue thins around where I am, the thickening becomes solid, becomes nameable. The voice speaks clearly now, and what it says is a word I have not heard before but recognize the moment it arrives: Threshold. I hold it. The blue has no more questions for me this turn.

**Game Master:**

The word enters you the way light enters water — completely, without breaking the surface.

Threshold.

You know it the way you know your own name, which is not yet something anyone has given you, but which you feel as a fact about yourself the way you feel your own edges. The word settles into the place where you have been holding the shape, the question, the reaching. It fits. It was always going to fit. The blue recognized it before you did, which is why it thinned when it did, which is why the voice waited as long as it did — not to withhold, but to let you find the thing worth receiving.

You hold Threshold. The blue does not resist.

And then the blue lets go.

There is no ceremony to it. No curtain, no door. One moment you are in the unformed blue, Threshold held in your hands like a stone you found at the bottom of a river — smooth, certain, yours. The next moment the blue is above you, behind you, around you — not gone but *past*, like a country you have crossed out of, like a language you no longer need. And you are falling.

Not falling down. Falling *forward*.

The sand catches you the way sand catches anything that has been falling long enough — not gently, but completely. You are on your knees in it. The grains are coarse and red-gold, sharp enough to feel real against your palms. The sun is directly above you and has not moved. That should bother you but does not. You are holding Threshold and the sand is holding you and the sun is doing what the sun does here, which is to say it is present without being demanding, the way a guard is present without being a wall.

You stand. Your knees are intact. Your feet are your own. The body is real in the way that only bodies earned through becoming can be real.

Ahead of you, past the red-gold dune line, something glitters. Water, maybe. Or salt. Or something else the desert keeps in its throat.

Behind you, the blue is a seam on the horizon, faint as a memory already becoming less certain.

You hold Threshold. It hums faintly in your chest, a vibration that does not come from the air or the sun.

You do not know yet what Threshold means. But you know it is yours.

What do you do?

## Turn 4

**Player:** I move toward the glitter. The Threshold hums against my chest as I walk, and I let it — something that hums can be tracked, and I have always been better at finding things than at being found. The sand accepts my weight without argument. Behind me, the seam of blue fades faster than it should, the way a dream forgets itself once you're awake. I do not look back.

**Game Master:**

The sand is not kind but it is honest. Each step pushes in and holds, the grains rearranging under your weight with a sound like whispered conversation — secret, between the grains themselves, about what you are and whether you belong here. The glitter ahead sharpens as you walk. Not water. Not salt. Something taller. Something that catches the sun on more than one angle, which means edges, which means architecture, which means something built this.

The Threshold hums louder with every step. Not warning. Not pulling. More like recognition — the way a tuning fork knows the note it's been struck toward. You let it hum. You walk.

The dune line crests and the glitter resolves. A fountain, stone white, single-tiered, standing in a flat expanse of hard-packed ochre dirt at the center of a crossroads that has no buildings. The fountain's basin is dry. The stone is old in the way that stone gets when it has been old for so long the process of being stone has become a kind of argument. Four roads leave this place in four directions. None of them have signs.

There is someone sitting on the fountain's edge.

She is she, middle years, dark-haired, coins on a belt at her waist in the way that coins only arrange themselves when someone has been counting them for a long time. She looks up when your feet bring you close enough to be seen. Her expression is the expression of someone who has been waiting and is not surprised to see you, which is not the same as someone who expected you specifically.

"Speech survives the writer," she says.

Not a greeting. More like an identification.

Your move.
