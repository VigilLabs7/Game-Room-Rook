You drift in the blue. Not water-blue, not sky-blue — the blue of something that has never been anything yet, the blue before color was sorted into names. There is no orientation in it, no up pulling at you, no floor below. You exist, which is the only miracle available here, but the existence has no shape you could draw. You are holding on to something. The only thing keeping you from dissolving into the unformed blue. A voice comes from beyond the blue, from above it, and it says something that makes the thing you're gripping feel suddenly like it could grow into a shape. The blue softens around the edges of what you are becoming.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold tighter. The voice speaks and the blue reshapes around what I'm becoming — but a thought surfaces: who is speaking? I open my mouth in the unformed dark and ask, quietly, *who are you?*

**Game Master:**

The question dissolves before it finishes leaving your mouth. The blue takes it the way the blue takes everything — completely, without residue. The voice that had spoken from above does not answer. The blue itself answers, reshaping around your doubt, around the question you asked, pressing harder into the edges of what you are becoming.

You feel yourself thin at the edges. The place where the question was — where the reaching-toward-something happened — the blue moves in to fill it. For a moment there is almost nothing there. Almost not-you.

You grip tighter. The memory of the voice, the shape it promised, the sense of something growing into a form instead of dissolving — you hold that. You hold it the way you held the first thing, when the blue was all there was and you were only the fact of existing.

The blue thins. The edges of you come back. You are still here.

The question cost you something. The blue took it and gave you back this: still holding, still unnamed, still becoming. The blue does not answer who is speaking. It only answers that something is, and that you are still reaching for it, and that the reaching is what keeps the edges of you where they are.

You hold on.

## Turn 2

**Player:** I do not ask again. The question cost me — I felt myself thin at the edges when the blue took it. Instead, I hold on and wait. I watch the place where the voice came from. The blue softens around the edges of what I am, and the voice pauses, as if waiting for me to find the question it will not take.

**Game Master:**

The blue notices that you are noticing it. That is enough to change what happens next.

You do not ask. The blue reshapes around your not-asking the way water reshapes around a stone that has stopped swimming — pressing in, finding the edges, doing the work the stone no longer does. You feel the thinning again, the soft pull at the margins of what you are. But you do not pull back from it. You hold where you are and let the blue come to you.

The voice pauses. For a long moment the blue holds its breath, and you hold yours, and the shape you have been becoming waits in the stillness between you. The voice has no face and no direction, but you can feel it working — as if it is building something just out of reach of your becoming, something that needs your asking to become real.

Then it comes. Not words. Not yet. A pressure in the blue itself, a thickening, a sense that something is trying to arrive at the edge of your held-and-waiting self. The blue goes momentarily thick as syrup, and in that thickness you feel something that might be a question shaped like a key, or a key shaped like a question — not given to you, but held in the blue, waiting for you to reach.

You feel the shape of it. You cannot quite touch it. The blue presses closer, the way a room presses closer when everyone has stopped speaking and is waiting for one person to say the thing that will change the room.

What do you do?
