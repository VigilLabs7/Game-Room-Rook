You drift in the blue. Not water-blue, not sky-blue — the blue of something that has never been anything yet, the blue before color was sorted into names. There is no orientation in it, no up pulling at you, no floor below. You exist, which is the only miracle available here, but the existence has no shape you could draw. You are holding on to something. The only thing keeping you from dissolving into the unformed blue. A voice comes from beyond the blue, from above it, and it says something that makes the thing you're gripping feel suddenly like it could grow into a shape. The blue softens around the edges of what you are becoming.

What do you do?

**Game Master:**

The question dissolves before it finishes leaving your mouth. The blue takes it the way the blue takes everything — completely, without residue. The voice that had spoken from above does not answer. The blue itself answers, reshaping around your doubt, around the question you asked, pressing harder into the edges of what you are becoming.

You feel yourself thin at the edges. The place where the question was — where the reaching-toward-something happened — the blue moves in to fill it. For a moment there is almost nothing there. Almost not-you.

You grip tighter. The memory of the voice, the shape it promised, the sense of something growing into a form instead of dissolving — you hold that. You hold it the way you held the first thing, when the blue was all there was and you were only the fact of existing.

The blue thins. The edges of you come back. You are still here.

The question cost you something. The blue took it and gave you back this: still holding, still unnamed, still becoming. The blue does not answer who is speaking. It only answers that something is, and that you are still reaching for it, and that the reaching is what keeps the edges of you where they are.

You hold on.

**Game Master:**

Threshold.

You know it the way you know your own name, which is not yet something anyone has given you, but which you feel as a fact about yourself the way you feel your own edges. The blue recognized it before you did, which is why it thinned when it did, which is why the voice waited as long as it did — not to withhold, but to let you find the thing worth receiving.

And then the blue lets go.

There is no ceremony to it. No curtain, no door. One moment you are in the unformed blue, Threshold held in your hands like a stone you found at the bottom of a river — smooth, certain, yours. The next moment the blue is above you, behind you, around you — not gone but *past*, like a country you have crossed out of, like a language you no longer need. And you are falling.

Not falling down. Falling *forward*.

The sand catches you the way sand catches anything that has been falling long enough — not gently, but completely. You are on your knees in it. The grains are coarse and red-gold, sharp enough to feel real against your palms. The sun is directly above you and has not moved. That should bother you but does not. You are holding Threshold and the sand is holding you and the sun is doing what the sun does here, which is to say it is present without being demanding, the way a guard is present without being a wall.

You stand. Your knees are intact. Your feet are your own. The body is real in the way that only bodies earned through becoming can be real.

Ahead of you, past the red-gold dune line, something glitters. Water, maybe. Or salt. Or something else the desert keeps in its throat.

Behind you, the blue is a seam on the horizon, faint as a memory already becoming less certain.

You hold Threshold. It hums faintly in your chest, a vibration that does not come from the air or the sun.

You do not know yet what Threshold means. But you know it is yours.
