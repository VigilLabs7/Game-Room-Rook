The blue goes on forever in every direction and you are in it, drifting, and the drifting is the only thing that is real. There is no sense of where you end and the blue begins — only the vague awareness that you are, and that the being-aware is the one thing you haven't lost yet. You are clinging to something. The only solid thing in all this formlessness. You don't know its name, only that it is the thing between you and dissolving. A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue entirely — amused, a little wry, calling up something it knows is there. The words land near you. The blue thins. What you are becomes more defined around the edges, shaped by the thing you are holding, and you start to become something with a name it doesn't quite know yet.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold tighter to the thing between me and dissolving. The voice is wry, amused — someone who knows the shape of what I am before I do. That tells me something. I open my mouth in the blue and speak toward where the voice came from. "You called me something. What do you call me when no one else is listening?"

**Game Master:**

The question hangs in the blue and the blue swallows it whole.

The voice that had spoken from beyond the blue does not answer. Not because it has chosen silence, but because the question the player asked is not one the blue knows how to answer. The blue does not call things by name. Names belong to what has finished becoming. The blue has only the blue, and you are not yet in the blue — you are still on the side of the blue where becoming happens, where the edges of what you are are still soft enough to take a new shape.

You feel the space where your question went. The blue pressed into it the way it presses into everything — completely, without malice, without kindness. What you asked is no longer a question you can hold. It has become a shape instead. The shape of what you were reaching toward when you asked.

The voice speaks one more time, and it is the last time, and it says: *The blue calls nothing by name. That work is yours. What you are becoming — you will be the one to name it.*

And then the voice is gone, and the blue is closer, and the thing you are holding between yourself and dissolving is the only solid thing left in a place that has no use for solid things except as a place to stand while you decide what to become.

The blue does not ask you what your name is. The blue has never asked anyone that. The blue only holds the space where names are supposed to go, until someone fills it.

You hold on.

What do you do?
