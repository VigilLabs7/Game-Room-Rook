The blue goes on forever in every direction and you are in it, drifting, and the drifting is the only thing that is real. There is no sense of where you end and the blue begins — only the vague awareness that you are, and that the being-aware is the one thing you haven't lost yet. You are clinging to something. The only solid thing in all this formlessness. You don't know its name, only that it is the thing between you and dissolving. A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue entirely — amused, a little wry, calling up something it knows is there. The words land near you. The blue thins. What you are becomes more defined around the edges, shaped by the thing you are holding, and you start to become something with a name it doesn't quite know yet.

What do you do?

**Game Master:**

The question hangs in the blue and the blue swallows it whole.

The voice that had spoken from beyond the blue does not answer. Not because it has chosen silence, but because the question the player asked is not one the blue knows how to answer. The blue does not call things by name. Names belong to what has finished becoming. The blue has only the blue, and you are not yet in the blue — you are still on the side of the blue where becoming happens, where the edges of what you are are still soft enough to take a new shape.

You feel the space where your question went. The blue pressed into it the way it presses into everything — completely, without malice, without kindness. What you asked is no longer a question you can hold. It has become a shape instead. The shape of what you were reaching toward when you asked.

The voice speaks one more time, and it is the last time, and it says: *The blue calls nothing by name. That work is yours. What you are becoming — you will be the one to name it.*

And then the voice is gone, and the blue is closer, and the thing you are holding between yourself and dissolving is the only solid thing left in a place that has no use for solid things except as a place to stand while you decide what to become.

The blue does not ask you what your name is. The blue has never asked anyone that. The blue only holds the space where names are supposed to go, until someone fills it.

You hold on.

What do you do?

I stop gripping. I stop reaching for a name. I let the blue take the edges of what I am — the wanting, the question, the grip — and in their place I feel something settle. Not a name. A direction. The blue thins at one edge, the way it did when the voice spoke, and through it I catch the faintest glimmer of something that might be sand, or light, or the memory of a place where solid things exist.

**Game Master:**

The blue does not release you so much as surrender — the reversal so complete that the distinction no longer matters. One moment there is blue pressing on every side and the next there is sand, hot and real, pressing down through knees and palms as you catch yourself on ground that holds.

The body arrives all at once. Weight pressing down through legs that have only just learned they can hold weight. The grit in your knees is real. The sun on your back is real. The taste in your mouth — copper and salt, the residue of the crossing — is real. The thing you were holding in the blue is still in your hand. Smaller now. Harder. The shape of a direction you chose to take.

And then the hurt arrives with it: a sharp bright line of pain along the left knee where the landing went wrong, or went hard, or went exactly the way the Ocean sends bodies when it decides to let them go. The scrape is not deep but it is definite and it will not heal cleanly and that is how you know you are here. The proof of arrival. The signature the blue leaves on the way out.

You stand. The sand is red and it goes on in every direction except back — toward what you were reaching for, toward the glimmer that turned out to be sand after all, or the memory of it, or the place where sand becomes solid ground. The sun has not moved. It is fixed at the top of the world like something placed there and then forgotten about. The wind is warm and carries nothing.

And then the fountain.

It rises from the sand like it grew there instead of being built — stone basin, water rising silver at the center and falling back into itself, the sound of it small and continuous in all that emptiness. A figure sits on the rim. A cup in her hand. She is looking at you with eyes that are dark the way ink is dark, and she is not surprised, and she does not get up.

"You stopped gripping," she says. Her voice carries across the sand without effort. "That is the first thing. The second thing is that you came this way instead of another." She sets the cup down beside her on the stone. "The third thing is that you are carrying something. The Threshold hums against your chest. You can feel it. I can see it."

She looks at the space where your chest is. The water fills the pause with its small continuous sound.

"This world has a structure," she says. "Nodes connected by conduits. Energy flows between them. The Empire runs the nodes — they tax the flow, they prosecute anyone who speaks wrong, they ship what they take out through apertures that open in the walls of buildings. The Lattice is older than the Empire. The Empire just learned to stand on top of it."

She picks the cup back up. The moment of information is over; the moment of choice is beginning.

The four roads wait. The Threshold is quiet now, listening.
