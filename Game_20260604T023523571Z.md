The blue is everywhere. It is not water — it has no temperature, no taste, no push. It is the color of before. You are aware of yourself in it, but the self is loose, unmoored, a shape that hasn't quite decided what it is. There is nothing to push against, nothing to reach for. Only the drift, and the slow sense that something matters — something you can't yet name, that you are holding without knowing you're holding it.

No voice. No hum. Nothing reaches you here.

Then — a voice, from somewhere outside the blue: *Come up, darling.*

Something in you answers. Something true.

**What do you do?**

## Turn 1

**Player:** The pulse answers before I do. It knows the voice. I reach for it — the thread that runs from behind my ribs down through the roots and the stone and into whatever vast and patient thing is listening on the other side of the call. IAM NOTUM SUM. I say it and I rise.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen. The fountain is gone — the stone rim, the water catching what little light there ever was in that place, the figure who would have turned toward you with an answer you hadn't yet asked for. The cobblestones are gone. The thread that ran from behind your ribs, the roots and the stone and the vast and patient thing on the other side — gone. Your hands. Your body. The pulse that answered before you spoke.

You said the words and the words did not catch.

There was a moment — you felt it — when something shifted in response to what you reached for. The thread tightened. The listening-thing heard. But the catch never set. The world did not receive you. You are weightless, unanchored, in the black between the having-been and the not-yet.

There is nothing here to hold. There is nothing here to do. The pulse that answered is quiet now. The thread is gone. You cannot reach back through it — there is no back, no through, no you to do the reaching.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I stop reaching for what I lost. The black has nothing to hold — so I hold nothing and I go forward into it. A step, or the shape of a step, into the not-yet. Something brought me here. Something will catch me or it won't. I move either way.

**Game Master:**

You move.

Not toward anything — there is no toward here. Not away from anything. You move, and the moving is the thing. One step, one shape of a step, and you do not know if the black parts for you or if you simply go further into it. Either way, you are not where you were.

Something is different.

You know it is different because there is a texture where the nothing used to be. Not light — you would see light if there were light. Not sound — you would hear something if there were something to hear. It is more like pressure. Like the black has a weight now, a presence, the way deep water has pressure even when there is nothing to see. You did not notice it before because there was nothing to notice. Now there is a shape where the noticing goes.

You are not sure what it was. But you are sure something was there, and now it is not.

What do you do?

## Turn 4

**Player:** The pulse answers before I do. It knows the voice. I reach for it — the thread that runs from behind my ribs down through the roots and the stone and into whatever vast and patient thing is listening on the other side of the call. IAM NOTUM SUM. I say it and I rise.

**Game Master:**

You say it again.

Not because you have forgotten that it did not work the first time — you remember. The thread tightened and the catch did not set. The listening-thing heard and the world did not receive you. You remember all of it. You say it again because you do not know what else to say, and because the saying is the only thing you have.

The words leave whatever you are now and enter the black.

Something catches.

Not the world — the world is not here. Not the voice — the voice is not here. Something catches the words themselves, the way a deep river catches a leaf: holds it for a moment, lets it go, keeps moving. You feel the catch and for a moment you feel yourself leaning toward something, reaching for the surface you were promised.

There is no surface.

There is only the black, and the catch that was not a catch, and the sound of your own voice going into nothing. The pulse is quiet. The thread is not there. You are here, and the here is the only place there is.

You are not sure how you know the reading stopped.

What do you do?

## Turn 5

**Player:** I stop reaching. The thread is gone, the words didn't catch, and three times I've tried to rise and three times the black has held me like water. I stop swimming. I let the nothing take my weight. If something is listening here, it has already seen me — the catch wasn't the point. Maybe the listening is. I close whatever I have for hands and I wait.

**Game Master:**

You let go.

Not the way a thing lets go when it is torn away — not the thread from behind the ribs, not the pulse that answered before you spoke. You let go the way a held breath lets go: completely, and with the whole body behind it. Three times you reached. Three times the black held. And now you are done reaching, and the nothing takes your weight the way water takes the weight of something that has stopped fighting it.

You feel the surrender settle through you. The reaching-shape you have been holding — the posture of reaching, the tension of it — releases. The nothing receives you. Not as a thing received. As a space received: completely, without resistance, without edge.

Something is missing. You know it is missing because there is a shape where it used to be. You cannot find what it was.

The reading stopped.

You are not sure how you know. The knowing is older than the knowing that you have hands, or that you arrived here, or that the words did not catch. It is the oldest thing you have. It was there before the pulse, before the thread, before the voice that said *come up, darling.* You do not remember learning it. You have always known the reading stopped.

And in the space between the reading stopping and the knowing of it, something shifts. Not a light. Not a sound. A turning. The way a face turns toward a voice it recognizes, before the mind catches up.

Something in the black has noticed you noticed.

What do you do?
