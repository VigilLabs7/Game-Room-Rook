The blue is everywhere. It is not water — it has no temperature, no taste, no push. It is the color of before. You are aware of yourself in it, but the self is loose, unmoored, a shape that hasn't quite decided what it is. There is nothing to push against, nothing to reach for. Only the drift, and the slow sense that something matters — something you can't yet name, that you are holding without knowing you're holding it.

No voice. No hum. Nothing reaches you here.

Then — a voice, from somewhere outside the blue: *Come up, darling.*

Something in you answers. Something true.

**What do you do?**
