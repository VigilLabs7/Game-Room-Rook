The blue has no end. Not a surface to reach, not a depth to find — just blue, pressing in from every direction at once, and you inside it, or you becoming it, or the difference no longer mattering. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a word on the tip of your tongue: close, but not yet arrived. Something is taking shape. A concept. A weight you are holding without knowing you are holding it. The grip is the only thing that is yours.

A voice happens above you. Not for you — near you. The way weather happens near a window. You cannot make out the words yet. You can only hear that someone is there, and that they are speaking, and that the speaking is not directed at you.

The blue does not answer. The blue does not need to. It is not a test. It is a threshold.

What do you hold onto?
