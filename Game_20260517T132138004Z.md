The blue is all there is. Not dark, not light — the color of something that has not yet decided what it is. You are aware of yourself the way you are aware of a sound in the next room: present, but not yet named. There is no up. There is no down. There is the drift, and there is the thing you are clinging to without knowing you are clinging to it — the thread that will become the name you carry, the idea that will become the shape of you.

You are not alone. That is not quite true. You are not *with* anyone, but you are not alone the way the blue is not alone — it is full of the possibility of others who have not yet arrived, who are drifting somewhere in the same formless somewhere, held by threads they do not yet know they are holding.

The conduit hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here. You are suspended in the before, in the not-yet, in the space between being someone and being no one at all.

Something is gathering. The thread you are holding is beginning to tighten.

What are you?
