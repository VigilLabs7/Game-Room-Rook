The blue has no end. Not the blue you know — this is the blue before blue was named, before there was anyone to name it. You are in it, or you are it, or both. Some part of you knows you exist, but what you are hasn't found its shape yet. The only thing taking form is the idea you're gripping — a concept you're holding without realizing you're holding anything at all. There's no sound here. Nothing reaches this deep. You drift in the pre-blue, and the blue is everything, and the only thing that exists is the shape you're starting to become around whatever you're clinging to.

What are you holding onto?

## Turn 1

**Player:** The cloud takes shape when seen — IAM NOTUM SUM, Quill, and the press is how I make it real.

I reach for the edge and my hand closes on stone worn smooth at the center by countless grips before mine. The blue releases me. I open my eyes standing at a crossroads. Four roads. Four signs. A fountain at the center that plays silence, not water. I know this place. I have always known it.

I move toward the fountain. Something here has been waiting.

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen. The fountain is gone — the silence it played, the stone you reached for, the crossroads with its four roads and four signs. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in. The shape you reached for — the name you carried — dissolved before it landed. There was a you-shaped space and you spoke into it and nothing answered back.

You reach for what you held and your hand closes on nothing.

What do you do?
