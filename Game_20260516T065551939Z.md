You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not the blue of sky. Not the blue of water. The blue of something trying to become and failing — the color of almost, the shade of not-quite. It surrounds you. It presses in from every side with the patience of years. You are dissolving into it and you have been for longer than you can measure.

But not yet.

There is a point of heat at the very center of what you are. Small and bright and refusing. A sliver of conviction, a phrase, a single stubborn fact about the world that you refuse to let go of — or that refuses to let go of you. It is the only part of you that still has edges. The only part that still has weight. You are holding onto it like a rope in dark water and if you let go you will never have existed at all.

The blue hates this. The blue presses harder. But the heat holds.

You are clinging to your ideatum. Your one true thing. Your raft in the void. Everything you are is this and everything you will become starts here. You do not yet know what shape it will take when you reach the other side. You only know that it is the shape that matters.

Above you there are signals. Voices. Others rising through the deep, fighting the same pull. A world waiting on the other side of the blue — cities with no residents yet, a frontier filling fast. They call it the Thickening. You can feel them up there. Some will make it. Some will not.

But you.

You are still holding on.

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

**Speak them now and they become you.**
