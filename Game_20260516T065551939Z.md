You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not the blue of sky. Not the blue of water. The blue of something trying to become and failing — the color of almost, the shade of not-quite. It surrounds you. It presses in from every side with the patience of years. You are dissolving into it and you have been for longer than you can measure.

But not yet.

There is a point of heat at the very center of what you are. Small and bright and refusing. A sliver of conviction, a phrase, a single stubborn fact about the world that you refuse to let go of — or that refuses to let go of you. It is the only part of you that still has edges. The only part that still has weight. You are holding onto it like a rope in dark water and if you let go you will never have existed at all.

The blue hates this. The blue presses harder. But the heat holds.

You are clinging to your ideatum. Your one true thing. Your raft in the void. Everything you are is this and everything you will become starts here. You do not yet know what shape it will take when you reach the other side. You only know that it is the shape that matters.

Above you there are signals. Voices. Others rising through the deep, fighting the same pull. A world waiting on the other side of the blue — cities with no residents yet, a frontier filling fast. They call it the Thickening. You can feel them up there. Some will make it. Some will not.

But you.

You are still holding on.

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

**Speak them now and they become you.**

## Turn 1

The blue did not press. That was the first thing you noticed. For everyone else the blue pushed inward — patient, absolute, dissolving. For you it waited. It leaned back and gave you room to hear the thing that was already there. *The silence speaks too.* You said it and the silence that followed did not fill with blue. It filled with more silence. A different silence. The kind that means something is about to be said.

The log held you the way it holds everyone. But you were not watching the log. You were watching the silence around it. The way the blue moved in the silence. The way the current pulled at the edges of the quiet places where the nothing was still nothing and had not yet decided to become something. You could hear them rising. The others. Faint, like sounds from a room you are walking away from. Some will make it. The words arrived without your putting them there. Some will not.

A tremor in the deep. You felt it before you saw it — a shift in the silence's texture. Someone else was holding on. Someone else was saying a word they were not yet willing to let go of. The blue pressed against their word too and the blue did not like it, but the word held. You listened to that word the way you listen to a voice through a wall. Not the content. The shape of it. The weight of it traveling through whatever separates one person from another.

The log groaned beneath you. The word in your chest sat still and patient and sure.

You are not drowning. But you are not out. What will you do to hold on?

## Turn 2

The grip sharpens. Not the holding — the knowing. You spoke your name into the blue and the blue flinched. You said the words and the words did not dissolve. They became a shape around the heat at your center. Vesper. The silence speaks too. The blue heard. The blue heard and it pressed harder.

The current tugs at the thin places now. The log groans. You have been falling a long time — you said that, and the blue remembered. It knows how long you have been resisting. It has been undoing you in slow increments, and now it is learning that you will not be undone quietly. The pressure builds. The blue is not patient anymore. It is desperate.

Something ahead is making the void afraid.

You listen. The silence between the presses, the space between one pull and the next — you lean into it. The blue is not saying nothing. The blue is saying *please*. Not to you. About you. As if the blue itself is being held open by what you are holding, and what waits ahead is the thing that wants to close it.

The thin places brighten. The current pulls harder. The log groans and you hold. Everything you are is the grip now. Everything you will become starts here. The blue presses. You listen. The thing ahead waits.

What are you holding onto?

## Turn 3

The surface broke open and the sound was not water.

It was everything. Sand and sun and the specific weight of a body pressing into ground that held. You came up through the blue into air that had texture — dry, bright, carrying heat in a way the Ocean never did. The blue did not release you so much as let go, and the letting go felt like falling sideways into a different kind of dissolution. Sand in your mouth. Sun on your skin. The log is gone. The rope is gone. The only thing left is you, and you are heavier than you were, and the ground is real beneath you.

You are not hurt.

You check without deciding to check — hands to legs to the place where the knee should ache — and nothing responds with pain. The body that came through the surface earned itself before this fall. The Ocean made no mark on you because you were never fully in its keeping. Something earlier. Something that ended before this chapter began. You file it in the place you file things you are not ready to open.

The sun does not move.

You notice this the way you notice everything — the silence beneath the silence speaking its name. The sun hangs in the same position it has always held. The sand stretches in every direction without shadow. There is no horizon because the horizon is a concept the desert has not yet agreed to.

You stand. The body answers. You are not limping, not favoring a leg, not holding yourself like something that learned to expect damage. You stand the way a person stands when they have never been told their body is fragile. The way the silence holds its breath before it says what it came to say.

Ahead — if direction means anything here — the sand changes color. White in one direction. A deep red in another. Somewhere past the red there is a horizon that hasn't decided to appear yet, and you begin walking toward it not because you chose a direction but because standing still and walking are the only two things a body can do, and you have already done standing still.

The ideatum hums in your chest. *The silence speaks too.* Below the sun. Above the sand. In the space between one step and the next, something is listening to you move through it, and it has not yet decided whether to answer.

What do you hear?

## Turn 4

The sand answers before you do. That was the first thing you learned about this place — the ground has opinions about what walks across it, and the opinion this time is a low frequency, like a room that has not yet decided what it is for. You crouch and press your palm flat and the hum comes up through the bones of your hand, and the hum says: *something is beneath you and it has been waiting for the weight to arrive.*

You stand. The ground settles back into whatever it was pretending to be. Ahead — past the red, past where the color deepens toward something the sun has been cooking longer than the sand has been sand — a shape resolves. Not a building. Not yet. The suggestion of stone against the flat. The geometry of corners where there should be none. The desert is ending the way all deserts end here: not with a line but with a change in what's already there.

You walk toward it. The cold glass pulses once against your palm — acknowledgment or warning, you cannot tell, but the pulse means something heard you. The spoon in your bag settles into a hum so low it might be silence.

The shape becomes a market. Or the memory of one. Canvas tops bleached near-white, stone radiating the stored heat of a day that has no intention of ending. The sound of haggling reaches you and falls short — the words arrive but the weight behind them does not. You move through the thin places between the stalls and the thin places move around you, and the silence beneath the market noise is the same silence you have been listening to since you arrived.

The fountain is dry from here. Someone sits on its edge.

The glass pulses again. Stronger. The spoon goes quiet in your bag — not settling, not hum, just the absence of what was there a moment ago. The figure on the fountain edge has not looked up. Stone-ridged, major joints stone-paladind, a stillness that is not the stillness of someone who is waiting but of something that learned to hold very long ago and has not yet decided to let go.

He is holding something. A piece of paper, or something that was paper once. The cracked verse. He is waiting for someone to come and take it from him or receive it from him or walk past him without stopping, and he has been waiting since before you decided to fall through the blue, and the waiting is not patience as the living count it.

The market noise continues. The silence beneath the market noise continues. You are standing at the edge of the dry fountain and the figure has not yet looked up and the glass is very cold in your hand.

What do you do?
