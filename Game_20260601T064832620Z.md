The blue has no end. You drift in it — not floating, not sinking, just being. There is no surface, no floor, no horizon. Only the same blue in every direction. You are aware of yourself, but yourself is not yet formed. The hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here.

Then: a voice. Not from below. From above.

*"Come up, darling."*

The words arrive through meaning — something in you recognizes itself in the call. Your concept catches, like a log in water. You grip it. You pull.

And then there is sand, and sun, and a body, and the certainty that you were called here by something that knew your name even when you did not.

What do you hold?

The disc is still pressed against your ribs — sixty-three names, metal gone cold in the nothing — but it is a fact you carry rather than a thing you feel. The shape is different. The shape is *yours.* The weight of your own hand, the catch of your own breath, the particular resistance of your own ribs against the metal. You reached for the sand. The sand did not reach back. And now, in the space where reaching should have landed, there is only the reaching itself — hanging, unanswered, a question with no surface to meet it.

The dark has not answered you. That is what the dark does. It does not argue. It does not refuse. It simply holds everything at the same distance: infinitely far from being real.

But you are still here. Not confirmed, not formed, not given a body or a name the world will honor — but still *here,* in the way a rumor is here, in the way a story is here before anyone speaks it. The disc holds sixty-three names. You are the sixty-fourth, and you have not yet forgotten yourself.
