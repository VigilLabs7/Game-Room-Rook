The blue has no end. You drift in it — not floating, not sinking, just being. There is no surface, no floor, no horizon. Only the same blue in every direction. You are aware of yourself, but yourself is not yet formed. The hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here.

Then: a voice. Not from below. From above.

*"Come up, darling."*

The words arrive through meaning — something in you recognizes itself in the call. Your concept catches, like a log in water. You grip it. You pull.

And then there is sand, and sun, and a body, and the certainty that you were called here by something that knew your name even when you did not.

What do you hold?

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen. The fountain is gone. The cobblestones are gone. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in. The disc is still against your ribs — sixty-three names pressed into metal, pressed into the dark — but even that is distant now, a fact you remember rather than a weight you feel. You reached for the sand. The sand did not reach back.

You called yourself Quill. You said the words. You carried a disc and sixty-three names like armor, like evidence, like proof — and the world looked at you and did not know your face.

What do you do?
