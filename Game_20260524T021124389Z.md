You follow at a distance — close enough to keep it in sight, far enough that the red of its eyes does not catch yours directly. The stone arm is warm against your ribs. Not pulling. Not warning. *Knowing.* The way it knows the route to a place it has been before in a life you did not live.

The corridor climbs. Stone walls give way to older stone — the kind that was here before the empire poured its foundations, the kind that remembers what the Lattice felt like when it was still building itself upward. The hum changes as you climb. Two octaves down from where it was in the holding cells corridor, and the change is sudden enough to register as a sound-shape, a pressure behind the sternum.

The guardbot stops.

Not slow. Not careful. A mechanical halt that reverberates through the floor — the kind of stop that means *destination reached* or *obstacle found.* You press yourself into an alcove and watch.

The aperture room door is open. Not the way doors open when someone is expected — the way doors open when something inside has decided to let the outside in. Light spills from the gap: blue-white, the same frequency as the bound man's eyes, but *wrong* in a way the stone arm reads before you do. The light is not steady. It flickers in a pattern that is not the Lattice's pattern.

The guardbot does not enter. It stands at the threshold and its red eyes dim further — not corruption, not empire-mandate. *Confusion.* Something is happening inside the aperture room that the guardbot was not programmed for.

The stone arm is warm and quiet at the same time. Warm because the Lattice is close. Quiet because the Lattice frequency is wrong — the same older hum the arm goes quiet around, but amplified, *active*, the way it only gets when the current is being manipulated rather than simply passed through.

A sound from inside the room. Not mechanical. Not human. Something between a hum and a voice, the way the Lattice sounds when it is carrying a name that is too heavy for the conduit. The empire harvests the shipping. Something upstream is trying to send more than the aperture was built to receive.

The guardbot's head turns. Not toward you — toward the door. Red eyes flicker once and hold.

The door waits.

What do you do?
