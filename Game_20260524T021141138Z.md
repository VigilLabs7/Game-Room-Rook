There is no direction in the blue. You are aware of this — the absence of direction, the absence of anything to push against. You float in a color that has no name because you have no name yet for yourself. There is only the slow accumulation of something that might become a self, built around an idea you cannot quite see. You are holding the shape of it without knowing you are the one holding it.

And then — a voice. From outside the blue, from somewhere above and apart. It knows your name before you do. It says *come* and the word is warm, the word is certain, and the idea inside you catches it like a hand catching a rope.

You begin to rise.

You are becoming. You are arriving. The blue falls away.
