The blue goes every way.

There is no surface. No floor. No horizon — only the same soft nothing in every direction, and you suspended in it, and the thing you are clinging to without yet knowing you are clinging to anything at all. It is small. It is shaped like a thought that arrived before language did. You hold it the way a body holds its own heat: automatically, without deciding to.

You are aware that you exist. That is all you are aware of. Not where you are — there is no where. Not who you are — the name has not yet formed. Only that something in you is still here, still holding on, still refusing to dissolve.

The blue is not cold. It is not warm. It is the color of almost-being.

And then — a voice. Not from below. Not from beside. From somewhere outside the blue entirely, somewhere you cannot point to because there is no direction here, no up or down to orient by.

*Come up, darling.*

Something in you lifts. Something in you answers. The thing you are holding — the small bright truth — catches the call like a branch catching wind, and suddenly you feel the blue beginning to thin.

What do you hold?
