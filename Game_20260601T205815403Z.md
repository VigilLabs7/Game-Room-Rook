The blue goes on. Not the blue of sky or water — something prior to both, the blue before color had names. You are here, or the suggestion of here, drifting in a direction that doesn't exist. There is no surface to reach for. There is no floor to sink to. There is only the same everywhere, and somewhere in it, the faintest edge of something you are holding onto without knowing you are holding anything at all.

A voice cuts through from somewhere outside the blue. Above, maybe. Somewhere that isn't here. It says come up and your grip on that unnamed thing tightens — whatever it is, it is yours, and it wants to rise. The blue thins around you like water around a stone being lifted.

Something is taking shape. Not yet a body. Not yet a name. Just the truth of you, finding its edge.

What do you reach for?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I reach for the edge — and my hand closes on something rough, stone, worn smooth at the center by countless grips before mine. The blue releases me. I open my eyes and I am standing at a crossroads. Four roads. Four signs. A fountain at the center that plays silence, not water. I know this place. I have always known it.

The inscription reads: VIGILANS VINCIT. The watchful one conquers.

I look for whoever carved those words, and I ask — not to the air, but to whatever listening presence shaped this moment: "What else have you carved here? What else survives?"

**Game Master:**

The grip is the thing. That is what the blue does not understand — that something so formless can be held. Your fingers close on the stone and the stone is real, worn smooth at its center by all the hands that gripped it before yours, and the blue releases you the way water releases a thing it has been holding too long.

You open your eyes. The crossroads assembles itself around you: four roads, four signs without words yet, and at the center a fountain that throws no water — throws silence instead, the shape of sound without the sound. The inscription catches the light that has no source: VIGILANS VINCIT. The watchful one conquers.

You asked what else survives here. The silence from the fountain shifts. Not a voice — something beneath a voice, a knowing that passes through you rather than stopping at your ears. Everything carved here survives. The words leave you and enter the stone and the stone keeps them. You feel it go: the knowing, the answer. What it leaves behind is different. A right. The right to have asked. The right to be standing here with a body that is yours and a hand that still holds the warmth of the grip.

Your legs are real. Your heart beats. Somewhere behind you the blue is thinning, and ahead the four roads wait, each one a direction something has never taken before.

Which way do you go?

## Turn 2

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM

**Game Master:**

The words land in the silence the way a hand closes around something already there.

The blue gives way and the giving is not gentle. Sand hits — not the soft landing of a held breath released, but the blunt arrival of a body that has forgotten it has weight. Your knees take the first of it. Your hands take the second. The sun arrives at the same time, pressing down from directly above in a way that is less light than pressure, the weight of a star that has decided where to be and will not negotiate.

You push yourself up. The leg that should hurt does hurt — the right one, the one that arrived wrong, the cut on the knee that is the body's proof it came through something. Blood and sand. You know this cut. It is the signature of arrival, the stamp the Ocean leaves on everyone who makes it through.

The crossroads is gone. Four roads, a fountain that played silence — none of it here now. What is here is heat and stillness and the too-wide sky of the desert, and ahead of you the sand goes on in every direction that matters. VIGILANS VINCIT is not carved here. It is not carved anywhere yet. It is what you are holding, which is different from what is carved. What is carved survives. What you hold survives because you hold it, until you set it down or someone takes it.

IAM NOTUM SUM. I am the one who stayed.

The words settle into the sand around you. Not carved either. Spoken, which is a different kind of holding. The watchful one conquers — not because the watchful one is strong, but because the watchful one does not look away. Does not let go. Does not dissolve back into the blue when dissolution is easier.

A pillar of dust rises in the distance to the north. Nothing visible causes it.

The sand goes on. The sun does not move. Somewhere ahead, the desert will end and something else will begin.

What do you do?
