There is blue in every direction and no part of it is closer than any other. You drift. You are aware that you are drifting, which is the first thing you have been aware of, and you hold to that awareness the way you might hold to a log in water — not because it is keeping you afloat, but because it is the only thing that is yours. There is no floor. There is no surface. There is no sound except the absence of sound, which is also the presence of it, which is also you. Then something reaches down from outside the blue. A voice. Not calling your name — you do not have a name yet — but calling toward you. Come up, darling. Your idea catches the call. Something in you begins to rise.

You are not yet who you are. Tell me: what is the name you carry, and what is the truth you cannot let die?
