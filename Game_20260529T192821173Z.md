The blue has no end. You are in it, and the being-in-it is all there is — no surface above, no floor below, no landmark in the monochrome that might tell you where you are or what you're made of. You know you exist, but the knowledge has no edges yet, no weight, no name attached to it. Something holds you here, the only thing between you and the dissolving — you can't say what it is, only that you are gripping it with everything you are. From somewhere far above the blue, a voice: not yours, not the blue's, from outside entirely. It says words you almost know. The grip tightens. The blue thins at the edges of what you are, and you begin to take shape around the thing you are holding.

What do you do?
