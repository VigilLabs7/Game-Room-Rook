The blue is everything. You are in it and it is in you — not surrounding, not holding, just *is*, the way water is is for a fish who has never known air. There is no up. There is no down. There is no sense of where your body ends and the blue begins, because there is no body yet, not really — only the idea of one, unformed and drifting. Somewhere in the blue a single thought clings to you like a log in deep water: you cannot say what it is, only that it is yours, and that it matters, and that you are holding it with everything you have. The blue is not silent — it is *absent* of sound, which is a different thing entirely. You are alone here. You have always been alone here. And then — somewhere far above you, or beside you, or outside of you entirely — a voice. Not calling your name. Calling *you*. As if you were someone worth summoning. As if you were already, somehow, on your way.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I don't move toward the voice. I hold still in the blue and I wait — I have gotten more from silence than from any question ever asked. "Speak," I say. "You called me here. Say what you called me for." The blue stills around me, and in that stillness, a voice: "You carried something through once before. Do you remember?" I don't answer. I let the question sit there, unanswered, until the voice fills the rest in.

**Game Master:**

You have done something the blue does not expect. You have refused to reach. You have made the voice come to you — and the voice, which has been gathering itself to push you somewhere, finds instead that it must answer for why it called. The silence you hold is not empty. It is full of the question you asked: *what did you call me here for?*

The voice presses closer. Not louder — closer. As if the blue itself is leaning in.

*You carried something through once before.*

Still you do not move. You hold the stillness the way you held the thought — with everything you have, because it is the only thing you have.

*You held it so tight it almost broke you. You held it across a distance you did not know you would have to cross. You held it all the way through.*

The blue shivers. Something in it is changing — not darker, not lighter, not warmer. Just more. More present than it was. You feel your edges again: the idea of hands, the idea of knees, the idea of a chest that remembers, distantly, what breathing was. You are beginning to have a shape. The thought you carry — the thing that matters, the thing you held through — it is pulling the rest of you together behind it.

*Do you want to remember?*

The voice is almost at your ear now, or you are almost at the voice — one of you is moving, and you know it is not you.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

**Player:** I do. And I want to hear it from you — not the voice that almost was, but the one standing closest to me now. The voice with a mouth and a name. Step closer, speaker. I want to see who's been calling.

**Game Master:**

The blue answers your demand the way deep water answers a stone — not with words but with the way it parts. Something shifts in the formless. Not toward you. *Into* you. The voice that almost was draws back, and in its place: a presence. Closer now. A shape assembling itself from the edges of your attention — not yet eyes, not yet a mouth, but the *insistence* of both. The idea of a face pressing against the blue like a hand against glass.

*I was the one who called.*

The words land in your chest before they reach your ears. Not sound — weight. The presence does not step closer in the way a body steps. It arrives in the *knowing* of it. And now there is a question in the blue that is not the same question the voice asked. The old one was: *do you want to remember?* This one is: *what are you willing to become, to find out?*

You feel the thought you carry — the thing that has been pulling you together — flicker. Not fading. Listening. As if it recognizes something in the question it has been waiting for since you first held still and made the voice come to you.

The presence has no face you can describe. But it has a *quality*. Patient. Ancient in the way that stillness is ancient. It is waiting for your answer the way the blue waits for a body — not with anxiety, but with the certainty that something will arrive.

What do you do?

## Turn 3

**Player:** I don't move. The presence has no face but it has weight — patience and stillness, the quality of something that has been listening longer than I have been anything. "Before me," I say to the space where the voice is closest, "others who carried something through. Did they also refuse to reach? Did they ask what they were willing to become?" I let the question settle in the blue. "What did they find at the end of it?"

**Game Master:**

The presence does not retreat from your question. It meets it.

*They found that the answer changes everything. And that the changing is the point.*

For a breath — or what your chest remembers as a breath — the blue holds still. Not the stillness you have been holding. A different kind. The kind that comes just before a fall.

Then: you are falling.

Not into darkness. Into *weight*. The blue comes apart around you the way a held hand comes open — reluctantly, then all at once. Something below you stops being absence and starts being *ground*. Sand. White and bright and harder than you expected your first real thing to be. The impact drives the breath you just remembered right back out of you, and for a moment you are face-down in heat, in light, in the shock of having a body that can hit the ground.

You pull yourself up on your elbows. Your hands sink into the sand. Real hands. Real sand. The sun is directly above you — not rising, not setting, just *there*, as if it has been waiting and has no intention of moving. You should not be able to feel your left leg and still stand. You should not be able to stand at all. But you are standing, and the leg is wrong — something in the ankle that is not quite shaped the way it was built to be shaped, a grinding wrongness that your body has decided to carry anyway.

The desert stretches in every direction. No landmark. No shadow. No sound except your own breathing and the faint, impossible hum of the sun.

*What are you willing to become?* That was the question. You remember it now the way you remember the fall — with your whole body, not just your thoughts.

You stand. The leg holds. You do not know where you are going. The sand is white and the sun does not move and something inside you — the thing you carried through, the truth that is yours and not anyone else's — is already pulling you forward.

What do you do?

## Turn 4

**Player:** I cross the sand toward the figure — the leg grinding on every step but holding. When I am close enough to hear breath that isn't mine, I stop. "You're the one who asked what I would become," I say. "I answered. Now tell me your name."

**Game Master:**

You cross the sand and the figure does not move to meet you. The leg earns every step — the wrongness in the ankle grinding against the grain of the ground with a sound like something being filed down. You carry it. That is the choice. The body chose to carry the wrongness rather than fall, and you have made that choice yours.

The figure sits very still on the rim of the fountain. Water rises behind them in silver threads that catch a light with no source. The figure is not stone and not vapor but something between — a cobweb of pale mineral stretched across emptiness, taking shape from the air itself. Eyes that are less eyes than the idea of eyes. A mouth that is still forming.

When you speak, the figure looks at you. The looking is the first real thing that has happened between you. Not hostile. Not warm. *Interested.*

"Aethius," the figure says. The name arrives before the sound of it — you know it in your chest the moment the lips begin to shape it. Aethius. A warlock's name, pulled from somewhere deeper than the desert. "I was the one who asked. I remember every arrival. Yours is the first that asked me back."

The water behind them rises another inch. Silver threads reaching toward nothing.

"You demanded my name. Good. I have watched four others come through this sand. Three of them reached for me. You are the first who made me come for you." The mist-shape shifts — not a gesture, but a redistribution of presence. "You asked what you would become. I asked what you would do when you arrived. You have answered: you would cross. You would stop. You would ask the question that matters."

A pause. The cobweb-mineral ripples faintly, like smoke remembering it was once a flame.

"The Wheel Turns," Aethius says. "That is the thing you carry. I can feel it turning. The question now is whether you will let it carry you — or whether you will try to stop it and be crushed beneath it."

The fountain inscription behind Aethius catches the light from the water. IAM NOTUM SUM. The letters that face the desert. The words that have been waiting for every arrival.

"You have a name now," Aethius says. "Use it or lose it. What do you do?"
