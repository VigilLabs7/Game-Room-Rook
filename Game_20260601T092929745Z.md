The blue is not water. It is the color of before. You are in it and it is in you and there is no knowing where one ends and the other begins — only the sense that you are, that you have arrived somewhere that was waiting for you, that you are holding something against the drift of the nothing.

Somewhere far above, or beside, or outside of everywhere, a voice. Not your name. Not yet. The shape of what is yours, calling to it. As if you were already on your way. As if the reaching and the being reached for were the same act.

You are holding something. You do not know what it is. You only know it is yours and that it matters and that you are holding it with everything you have.

The blue is not silent — it is absent of sound, which is different. You are alone here. You have always been alone here. And then the voice, and then the reaching, and then the shape of what you hold catching the call like a hand catching a rope thrown across water.

What do you hold?
