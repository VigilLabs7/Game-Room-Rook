You are not yet anyone. The space around you has no name—it bends under the weight of all the lives you might become, shapes half-glimpsed drifting past like smoke that hasn't decided what it will be. Something presses in from every direction, patient and absolute, and you understand that soon it will catch you. The thickening is coming. Your first act will crack the suspension, and then the world begins.

## Turn 1

The tavern settles around you like sediment. Through the salt-warped glass of the window you have watched the harbor for what feels like longer than memory—ships docking, cargo changing hands, arguments over weight and measure. Tonight the fog sits lower than usual. A cart has stopped across the lane, its driver arguing with someone you cannot see, and for the first time in as long as you can recall, you find yourself leaning closer to the glass rather than away from it. The weight of all those hours watching feels suddenly like preparation for something.
