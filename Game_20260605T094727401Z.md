The blue goes on. Not water — the word is wrong — but the feeling is close enough. You are in it, and it is in you, and there is nothing else. No floor. No surface. No sound except the not-sound of your own awareness, which is the only proof you have that you exist at all. Something flickers at the edge of the blue: a shape, a pressure, the ghost of a direction you might try to swim if you had arms that remembered how to swim. You are holding something. You don't know what it is yet. That is the whole of it.

A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue — not below you, not beside you, from above, from a place where the blue thins and the air might be different. It says *come up*. It says *darling*. You don't know who is being called. But the thing you are holding tightens, and you feel yourself rising.

What do you hold?
