You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue, and the blue is the blue of a door left open in a house that has no rooms — the blue of a welcome that was extended before anyone thought to ask who was being welcomed, or why, or what they would be asked to carry once they arrived. It is everywhere and it is nothing and it is patient the way erosion is patient, the way forgetting is patient, the way a question that will never be answered is patient in the asking.

You are not sure if you have hands. You might. The blue has not yet decided what to make of you. It is still working on it — still shaping and reshaping, still trying to find the form that fits the empty space where a person might someday stand. You feel yourself becoming less and more at once, less defined but more present, less certain but more here.

Somewhere above you — or below you, or beside you in a direction that does not have a name — there is a surface. A place where the blue ends and something else begins. You cannot see it. You have never seen anything. But you know it is there the way you know a word is there just before you speak it, the way a name is there just before it lands on someone's ears and becomes real.

You are holding onto something. A thread. A log. A conviction. You do not remember picking it up. You do not remember deciding to hold it. But your hands — if you have hands — are closed around it, and it is solid, and it is yours, and the blue has not yet found a way to take it from you.

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**
