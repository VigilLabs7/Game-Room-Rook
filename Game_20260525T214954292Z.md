The ledger is still open on the counter between you. The heading — *INVENTORY — SECONDARY STORAGE* — is still there. The nine Hayeks are still in a row. The shard is still glowing in your hand and the shopkeeper is still looking at it with a face that does not know which face it wants to be.

"Where did you find that?" he says. Not *where did you get it.* Where did you find it. As if it was lost. As if it was buried. As if it was put somewhere on purpose and forgotten about on purpose and the finding of it was always going to mean something he was not prepared for.

You hold the shard up. The blue light is between you now — you and him and the sentinel in the same room with something the sentinel does not have a file for.

"In the window," you say.

His hands press harder against the wood.

"In the window," he repeats. Not a question. A fact he is trying to make not be true. "Between the panes."

The patch holds its shape under the light. The sentinel is not speaking. The sentinel is not flickering. The sentinel is quiet in the way that things are quiet when they are out of their depth.

The shard is warm in your hand. The inscription reads IAM NOTUM SUM. The shopkeeper's face is doing something you have not seen it do before — the muscles around the patch are moving, are trying to move, as if the thing under the cloth is pressing against it from the inside. Not the sentinel's eye. Something else. Something the sentinel is not.

"I was here when they put it in," the shopkeeper says. His voice is his own. The sentinel has not spoken since you turned the shard. "I was here before the window. Before the shop. Before I had a counter and a ledger and a purpose." A swallow. The blue flickers once — a brief pulse — and goes still. "I don't remember who put it in. I don't remember why. I remember that it was warm when they put it in and I remember that I watched them seal the window around it and I remember that I have never seen it glow before."

The shard pulses once in your hand. The light it gives off is steady now, warm, directed — not filling the room but pointing at something. At the ledger. At the heading: *INVENTORY — SECONDARY STORAGE.*

The shopkeeper reads the light the way you read his face. Slowly. Then fast.

"It knows what it is for," he says. Quietly. To himself. "It knows what it was broken for."

What do you do?
