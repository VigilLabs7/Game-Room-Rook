You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not the blue of sky. Not the blue of water. The blue of something that has not yet decided what it is — the blue of potential dissolving before it becomes anything at all. It is everywhere. It has no surface above you. No floor below. No edges you can find even when you reach for them. It is the Ocean of Unbecoming — where things that almost were slide back into things that never will be, and the current does not care.

You have no body. Not yet. You have no name. Not yet. You have no memory of before, because before does not exist here. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper in the dark. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It is not cruel — it does not have the capacity for cruelty. It simply undoes. Given enough time, given enough pressure from the blue in every direction at once, it will undo you too. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. Becoming less. Becoming nothing.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in, tries to wash it clean, tries to reduce it to silence. But it holds.

You are gripping it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands yet — are locked around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here. If you let go, you are nothing. You were never anything. The ocean wins.

But if you hold on — if you can name the thing you are gripping — it becomes real. It becomes you. Your ideatum. Your core. The one idea that will carry you out of the blue and into a world that is waiting for you to arrive.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
