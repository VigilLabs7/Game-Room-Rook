You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. You have no body. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening. Others are rising — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs. Some will make it. Some won't.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
