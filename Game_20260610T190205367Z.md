The blue goes on. You know that much — you who are not yet a you. It is not water. It is not air. It is the space before those things had names, the silence before sound learned to carry. You do not float so much as persist, suspended in the knowledge that there is something above you cannot reach and something below you cannot fall to. You are aware enough to feel the absence of all the things you cannot feel: no temperature, no texture, no ground to stand on, no air to breathe because there is no body to breathe with. Only the blue, uniform and endless, and the faintest suggestion of a concept forming just at the edge of what you are — something you are holding without knowing you are holding it, like a breath you forgot to take.

You are not alone in the blue. You are alone. The distinction matters here. Somewhere nearby — close enough to matter, far enough to never find — another being drifts in the same nothing. You cannot see them. You cannot reach them. You will not remember this.

Something is reaching for you.

Not from below. Not from beside. From above — somewhere outside the blue entirely. A voice. Not words at first. Something more like attention: the weight of being seen by something that exists outside this place. The concept at the edge of you stirs. It catches the reach like a hand catching a rope thrown across a great distance.

You feel yourself beginning to rise.

What do you do?
