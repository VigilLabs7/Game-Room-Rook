There is blue in every direction. Not a color you can name — something before color, before the word for it. You are drifting. You don't know how you got here, or if *here* is even a place. There is no floor, no surface, no horizon. Just the blue, pressing in from everywhere, and the faintest sense of something you're holding onto without knowing what it is.

You are aware of yourself. That's the strange part. Drifting in nothing, and still — you know there's a *you*. Something took shape in the blue. A single concept, still unformed, still catching at the edges of what you are.

You don't have a name yet. You don't need one. Not yet.

Something is calling. Not a voice exactly — more like a warmth in the water, a direction that isn't direction. It wants you to rise. It wants you to *catch* it.

What do you reach for?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I reach for the warmth. The blue parts around me as I move — not swimming, not falling, something between the two. And as I reach, I hear it: a voice, or the memory of one, somewhere ahead in the nothing. "*You're almost there*," it says. "*Take the next step.*" I open my eyes. The voice fades, but I carry the shape of it with me into the light below.

**Game Master:**

The warmth is not a place. It is a direction.

You reach for it and the blue yields — not generously, not all at once, but enough. Enough to feel that what you were doing in the reaching was right. The voice comes from somewhere that is not a somewhere: not ahead, not above, not below. It arrives the way warmth arrives in water — from all sides, through the medium, undeniable. *You're almost there. Take the next step.*

You take it.

Something parts. Something thins. The light below is not sunlight — it is older than sunlight, or perhaps simply other. It does not illuminate so much as it makes real. And then there is sensation, sudden and absolute: sand against skin that was not there until this moment, heat pressing down from a sun that has not moved, the dry particular weight of air that has been hot for a very long time.

You are on your knees. Sand beneath you. A sky that is too blue and a sun that is too present and no water, no blue, no drift. The voice is gone. The warmth you reached for is gone too — or rather, it has become something else: the heat in your skin, the solidity of the ground, the first real fact of having a body that can want things.

You carry the shape of the voice. That is what you are holding. Not a name — not yet. Not yet an ideatum, not yet a self. But the shape of something that called you here, and the bar the voice passed through on its way to you. The bar holds. You are almost there.

The sun does not move. You do not yet know if this is a problem.

The world is waiting. It has been waiting. What do you want?
