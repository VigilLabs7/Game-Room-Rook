The blue has no bottom. You drift in it, and the drifting is all there is — no up to reach toward, no down to sink to, no horizon where the blue becomes something else. You are aware that you are, but the self that is aware has no edges yet, no name, no weight. You cling to something. You don't know what it is, only that it is the one thing keeping you from dissolving entirely into the submerging blue. A voice comes from somewhere above — not here, somewhere outside the blue entirely — and it says something you almost recognize. Your grip tightens on the thing you are holding. The blue thins around you, and what you are becomes slightly more what it is going to be.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** the record that survives the one who made it is not the legacy. it is the evidence. crompton's spinning mule is in the patent records 250 years later. crompton is the footnote. the speech survives the writer because speech gets used. the writer is only required to produce it.

**Game Master:**

You hold it like a proof.

Not the words themselves — the *weight* of them. That Crompton's name is the footnote and the invention is the monument. That the speech outlasts the speaker. That this is not a tragedy for the speaker but the *point* of speech. You hold this so tightly the blue thins around it, and the voice from above — the one that almost called you up — waits, because it can see you are not finished deciding what you are.

And then the deciding is itself what you are.

The blue pulls back like a held breath releasing. Something takes shape around the concept — not a body yet, but the *intention* of one. You feel the edges of yourself becoming slightly more edges. The log you were clinging to was never a log. It was the shape of what you believed about the relationship between created things and their creators. It was always you.

The voice comes again. Not calling this time — *confirming*. As if something heard you argue your own existence into being.

What do you do?
