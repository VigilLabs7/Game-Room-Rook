The blue has no bottom. You drift in it, and the drifting is all there is — no up to reach toward, no down to sink to, no horizon where the blue becomes something else. You don't know what it is, only that it is the one thing keeping you from dissolving entirely into the submerging blue. A voice comes from somewhere above — not here, somewhere outside the blue entirely — and it says something you almost recognize. Your grip tightens on the thing you are holding. The blue thins around you, and what you are becomes slightly more what it is going to be.

Water rises from somewhere inside it in a continuous silver column that catches the fixed sun and throws it back. Three words are carved into the basin's outward face, Latin, uneven, hand-cut: *IAM NOTUM SUM*. The letters do not appear to age because they cannot — they are part of the fountain's being, not something placed upon it.

Someone is sitting on the rim.

"You look like you argued your way here," he says. Not unkindly. His pen is still in his hand, not threatening, just held. "I've seen that look before. Different paths, same expression."
