You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in, tries to wash it clean, tries to reduce it to silence. But it holds.

You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer of existence where things have weight and light and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. A frontier filling up fast. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. You can feel them in the deep — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs, fighting the same current.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak the name you will carry, and speak your ideatum.**

The ocean leans closer.

Your hands are still white-knuckled around the log. Your name is still Vesper. Your ideatum is still the silence, and the silence is still speaking, and the ocean is still listening to see what you do next.
