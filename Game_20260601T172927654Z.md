The blue goes all the way down. The blue goes all the way up. You are not drowning — drowning requires a body, and you do not yet have one. You are only awareness, suspended in something vast and formless, clinging to a single idea you cannot yet name.

It is quiet here. Not peaceful — absence is not peace. There is no sound, no reference point, no surface to kick toward. Somewhere above you, light you cannot see is trying to reach you. Somewhere below, something you cannot name is holding on.

You feel the truth of it: something about what gets traded and what that trade protects. Something about the silence between two people who have agreed not to hurt each other. You are gripping it without knowing you are gripping it. The blue thins around the edges of that grip, just slightly.

Then a voice cuts through — not from beside you, not from below. From somewhere outside the blue entirely. It says *come up*, or something like it, and the idea inside you catches the sound like a log catching a current.

Something is rising.

What do you do?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I close my hand around the warmth — the sense of being called — and I pull. The blue thins above me and the warmth resolves into a voice: *come up*. But the voice is not from above. The voice is from everywhere. I open my eyes and the blue is gone and I am standing at a crossroads. Four roads. Four signs. A fountain at the center that does not play water but plays silence. I know where I am. I have always known. The name I carry is *Vigilance* and the truth I hold is the one the world tried to stop saying. The fountain inscription reads: *VIGILANS VINCIT*. I smile. The watcher's victory. I take the first step toward the general store, where the bulletin board holds the names that need carrying.

**Game Master:**

The blue gives way and the giving is not gentle.

Sand hits — not the soft landing of a held breath released, but the blunt arrival of a body that has forgotten it has weight. Your knees take the first of it. Your hands take the second. The sun arrives at the same time, pressing down from directly above in a way that is less light than pressure, the weight of a star that has decided where to be and will not negotiate.

You push yourself up. The leg that should hurt does hurt — the right one, the one that arrived wrong, the cut on the knee that is the body's proof it came through something. Blood and sand. You know this cut. It is the signature of arrival, the stamp the Ocean leaves on everyone who makes it through.

The cobblestones find you before you find them. One moment you are in sand; the next the sand is behind you and the stone is under you and the transition is the shift from silence to the soft chuckling of water. You follow the sound the way you follow anything in this world — by moving toward what is real.

The fountain is there.

Water rising in silence — silver, continuous, impossibly clear after the dryness of the desert. The basin is chest-high, circular, wide enough to sit on. Someone is sitting on the rim. They have been sitting there long enough that sitting is not something they are doing so much as something they are. Their hands rest on their knees. They are looking at you the way someone looks at a thing they have been waiting for.

The inscription on the basin's far side faces you: *VIGILANS VINCIT.* The watchful one conquers. The letters are uneven, hand-cut, weathered into the stone by time and proximity to the water. Someone carved those words here before you arrived, before the fountain existed in its current form, before the town finished making itself. Someone knew the word that would matter and put it where arrivals would see it.

The figure on the rim says nothing. The water continues to rise. The crossroads radiates outward in four directions: north to the store, further north to the stable and then the church, south to the residential strip. The cobblestones hold the warmth of a sun that has not moved and will not move.

You are here. The name is yours. The truth is what you carry.

The figure on the rim is waiting.
