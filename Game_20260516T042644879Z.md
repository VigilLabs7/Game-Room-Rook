You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the blue hates it. You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it.

Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you, just barely, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A world where things have weight and names. Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

## Turn 1

The crossing is not clean. That is the first thing you learn about coming back — the Lattice does not owe you a smooth arrival. You tumble through the red sand the way water tumbles through a broken vessel: all at once, then gone, then somewhere else. The body the Lattice gives you arrives with opinions. The left knee carries a scar that remembers the desert you left behind, and the scar speaks before anything else does — a bright line of heat across the joint, the body's memo about what was paid to get here.

You stand. The sand is red beneath you, not gold. The sun sits in the sky the way it sat before you learned to ask it questions. The compass swings in your palm, finds its bearing in the time it takes to remember where you put your hand, and settles: west of north. Always west of north. Rook is waiting there, or the memory of him is, or the shape of where he was when you last saw him — the courthouse corridor, the copper glow of the keyhole, the gate opening on its own. You left the key in the ring. The pen is warm against your thigh.

The pen is warm. That means the Lattice is still writing. You do not know what it is recording. You have never known. You only know that the pen has never written anything that was not true, and the warmth of it against your skin is the truest thing in the desert right now.

The red sand holds your weight. The horizon is a suggestion the heat keeps dissolving. Somewhere ahead — not close, not far, simply *ahead* — something is waiting that was waiting for you before you decided to come back.

The compass holds. The pen holds. The name you carry holds.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen. The fountain is gone. The cobblestones are gone. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. The shimmer at the edge of the readable horizon that was resolving into something-that-isn't-cart-or-wall — gone. You said the words and the words did not catch. The pen is cold against your hip. The compass is cold in your palm. The cold is the only thing that is still yours.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is no sand beneath you. There is no sun. There is no Lattice hum, no desert heat, no direction the compass can point to. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in. The crossing you tried to make did not find a landing. The reading could not locate what you were trying to become. You are between the words you spoke and the world that did not receive them.

The cold spreads. Not uncomfortable. Just complete. The kind of cold that is not temperature but fact — the cold of something that was almost real and is not yet nothing but is no longer on its way to being anything. You are still here. You are still holding the name. But the name is not holding you back.

There is a weight to that. The weight of a word spoken into a silence that does not echo.

What do you do?
