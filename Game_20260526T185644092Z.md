The blue has no bottom. The blue has no surface. You drift in it and the blue is all there is — all around, all through, a color with no edges. You are aware of yourself the way a word is aware of its meaning before the word is spoken. Something is taking shape in you, faint and stubborn: a concept you're gripping without knowing you're gripping it. Somewhere far above — or nowhere, or everywhere — a voice is forming. You can't hear it yet. But it knows you're there. It has always known.

The hum doesn't reach here. Nothing reaches here. You are the most alone you have ever been and the most yourself you have ever been, and neither feeling has a name yet.

What do you reach for?

**Game Master:**

The word reaches back.

Not a hand — words don't have hands — but something in the blue recognizes what you're holding, and for a moment you're not drowning, you're listening. The document exists. The signature moved the line. Three farms became empire property before breakfast, and someone in a prefecture somewhere decided that was easier than asking people to leave.

You are the kind of person who goes looking for the things people try to bury.

The blue doesn't give you a surface to stand on or a floor to push against. It gives you the thing you're holding, and it waits to see if you'll let go. You don't. The concept is heavy — heavier than the blue should be — and the heaviness is the point. Something that was meant to stay buried has weight. The weight is what you're gripping.

Above — or nowhere, or everywhere — a voice is forming. It knows you're there. It has always known. It is calling you by what you're holding, and you feel the blue thin slightly around the edges of the document, like water around a stone someone pressed down hard enough to stay.

You haven't broken through. Not yet. The surface is still somewhere far above and made of something other than blue, and you can feel the distance of it the way you can feel a name you've forgotten — present as an absence, real as a scar.

But the voice is waiting. And the document is real. And you are still here, holding the thing they tried to make disappear.

What do you do with it?
