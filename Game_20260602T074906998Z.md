The blue has no bottom. You drift and there is nothing to push against, no surface above, no floor below — only the same blue in every direction, the same weightless suspension, the same absence of reference. You are aware of yourself the way you are aware of a word you've forgotten: present but not graspable. Something is taking shape at the edge of you — not a thought, not a memory, something older and harder to name. A single concept you've been carrying without knowing you were carrying it. The blue is patient. It has been waiting for you to notice.

What are you holding onto? What truth brought you here — or what truth are you becoming?
