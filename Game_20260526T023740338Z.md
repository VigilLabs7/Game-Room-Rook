The courthouse door is behind you. You spoke the old words against its frame and the frame held them the way stone holds anything pressed into it: without comment, without change, without opening.

The old words open doors in the blue, where the self is still unformed and language has not yet learned its own edges. Here the self is already made and the words have already been spent. The courthouse door was never yours to open with IAM NOTUM SUM because you are already inside.

The corridor opens into the chamber.

The golems are waiting. Not waiting in the way creatures wait — alert, intent, wanting something. Waiting in the way stone waits: with the patience of things that were built to outlast the question of why. Their halberds are at port. Their heads are turned toward the aperture. They were facing forward when you entered the room. They are not facing forward now.

The aperture fills the far wall — a vertical ellipse of light, brighter than anything in the building, pulsing upward in a slow rhythm that matches nothing in your body. The walls around it are scorched in concentric rings, the way walls scorched by heat over long use look. The air tastes of ozone and distance. The hum is here — low, continuous, the sound of something moving through the aperture that is not light and not air and not anything you have a name for yet.

One golem's head turns. Not the full turn of attention — just enough. The voice comes low and grinding, stone grinding against stone in the jaw: *You carry a door. The aperture knows.*

The other golem does not turn. It does not need to. Its halberd is already angled.

What do you do?
