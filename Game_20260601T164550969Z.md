The blue goes all the way down. The blue goes all the way up. You are not drowning — drowning requires a body, and you do not yet have one. You are only awareness, suspended in something vast and formless, clinging to a single idea you cannot yet name.

It is quiet here. Not peaceful — absence is not peace. There is no sound, no reference point, no surface to kick toward. Somewhere above you, light you cannot see is trying to reach you. Somewhere below, something you cannot name is holding on.

You feel the truth of it: something about bars and what they hold. Something about the weight that doesn't break. You are gripping it without knowing you are gripping it. The blue thins around the edges of that grip, just slightly.

Then a voice cuts through — not from beside you, not from below. From somewhere outside the blue entirely. It says *come up*, or something like it, and the idea inside you catches the sound like a log catching a current.

Something is rising.

What do you do?
