The blue goes on. Not the blue of water or sky — the blue of before color, before the word had meaning. You are here, or you are the idea of here, and the distinction doesn't matter yet.

There is nothing to push against, nothing to reach for. Only the slow awareness of being, and the vague sense that you've held onto something without knowing what it is. A concept almost solid. Something that might, if you let it, become the shape of you.

Above is unreachable. Below is the same as here. The blue swallows everything equally.

You exist. You are not yet sure what that means.

What are you holding onto?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold the shape of a door. Not a door I've opened — a door I've walked through before, in another life, when I was still the one asking the questions. The blue shifts. Something in me remembers the weight of a hand on my shoulder, the taste of cheap wine, the sound of a name being given up. I let the shape take form. The blue goes on around it, but I am no longer formless. I am the space between two doors, and I know which one I want.

**Game Master:**

You choose, and the choosing is everything.

The blue doesn't tear — it exhales. One moment you are the idea of a person standing in the idea of a door, and the next moment something vast and indifferent hears you, and the floor you've been standing on becomes the floor that abandons you, and you are falling — not through space but through *commitment*, the way a held breath finally releases, the way a verdict arrives and cannot be taken back.

The sand hits you like a point of view.

You are on your back. The sky above is white-gold, the color of things that have never learned to be subtle. Heat presses down from everywhere at once — above, below, the air itself seems heavy with intention. Your hands sink into the grains when you move. Your mouth tastes like nothing you've had before, and also like something you've forgotten.

You sit up. The leg screams before you fully understand why.

Your left knee is open. Not deeply — a long scrape, raw and honest, the kind of wound that announces itself without apology. Blood and sand mix into a paste that catches the light. You look at it and you know: *this is real. This body is yours now, for better or worse, and it has already been marked by the crossing.*

The desert stretches in every direction without apology. White sand under a sun that has not moved since you arrived. No shadows to tell you which way is which, except by the quality of the heat — fainter behind you, where you fell from; denser ahead, where something pulls.

Ahead. That's the direction. The columns must balance — that's the shape you held in the blue — and a column needs both sides to stand. You've always understood this, even before the door. Some truths are older than the body that carries them.

A dark line on the western horizon. Not a shadow — a depth. A place where the sand stops and something else begins.

You walk toward it.

## Turn 2

**Player:** I walk toward the dark line. The sand grips my bleeding knee with every step and the desert leans close, pressing its heat against my ribs like a conspirator's whisper: *you chose this, you always chose this, you just don't remember choosing yet.* I let it speak. I keep walking.

**Game Master:**

The sand does not care what you remember. It grips and releases, grips and releases, a rhythm like breathing, like the tide it pretends not to be. Your knee paints a thin red thread behind you — evidence of passage for a world that will not ask your name for another few minutes yet.

The dark line resolves as you close on it. Not a cliff. Not a wall. A *change* — where the white sand thickens into something with texture, where the silence of the open desert gives way to a sound you almost recognize: the small, persistent conversation of water over stone.

You crest a rise and there it is.

The fountain stands at the center of a square of older architecture — cobblestones instead of sand, buildings that have learned to lean rather than fall. The water catches the light in a way that seems deliberate, like it knows it's being watched. And beside it, sitting on the fountain's edge with both hands flat on the stone, a figure in worn armor watches you back.

He is solid in the way that only people who have chosen to stay have ever been solid. His face has the weathered quality of someone who arrived here with a question and found that staying was the answer. He doesn't smile as you approach. He doesn't frown. He watches you the way a person watches something they've been waiting for without knowing they were waiting.

"Bad knee," he says. It isn't a question.

The heat of the desert still presses at your back. Ahead, the square opens in all directions — a general store with a faded sign, a stable with no visible horses, a church with a bell that doesn't ring, and a residential strip where the windows are dark. But all of that is future. All of that is what comes after.

Right now there is only the fountain, and the man beside it, and the way he looks at you like he already knows which column you came to balance.
