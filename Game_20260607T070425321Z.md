The blue is not water. You are not swimming. You are drifting — suspended in something that has no surface, no floor, no edge, no other presence anywhere in it. There is only you and the blue, and the blue has no temperature and no sound and no particular quality you can name, only that it is the color of before. Somewhere in it, without knowing you are holding on, you are holding on to something. A concept. A truth you cannot yet read. The blue shifts around it, thins perhaps, and you feel — without feeling — the faintest pull toward something above you that has not yet spoken.

You are not alone. You are simply not yet known.

What do you reach for?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I close my eyes. The blue is patient. I wait for it to speak again — and in the waiting, I ask: *Tell me, traveler — are you a being like me, or something else?*

**Game Master:**

You close your eyes and the blue does not change. There is nothing to see with them open and nothing to see with them closed. The color is the same. The patience is the same. The question you asked falls into the blue the way a stone falls into deep water — and like a stone in deep water, it does not reach the bottom. It simply stops, suspended, hanging in the nothing between what you meant and what the blue is.

The voice does not answer. There is no voice. There was never a voice. What you heard was the suggestion of a voice, the shape of a presence that the blue assembles from its own refusal to be empty. You reached for it and it reached back and now you are both still, the reaching and the not-answering, balanced in the same impossible depth.

You asked if it was a being like you. The question assumed there was something there to ask. The blue does not confirm and does not deny. The blue is not a being. It is the space between being and not-being, and you are suspended in it, and whatever you are holding — whatever truth you cannot yet read — has not yet grown heavy enough to pull you down or light enough to let you rise.

You asked. The blue is patient. The question hangs.

You hold on.

**What do you do?**

## Turn 2

**Player:** I reach toward the thin light. It parts. I drift through the opening and the blue spills closed behind me — and I am somewhere else now, somewhere with air and weight and the smell of old paper.

**Game Master:**

You reach and the blue opens.

It is the simplest thing in the world — you reach toward the thin place and it gives way, and you drift through the gap like a word slipping out of a sentence, and behind you the blue closes and seals and is gone as though it had never been. And then there is air, which is a shock because the blue had no air. And weight, which is a second shock because the blue had no gravity. And the smell of old paper, which is a third shock because the blue had no smell at all.

Sand takes you face-first.

The fall is not far — three feet, perhaps four — but weight makes everything faster and sand is not forgiving. Your hands break the worst of it. Your knees find the worst of it anyway. The cut on your left one opens on something sharp just beneath the surface and the pain is immediate and specific and absolutely real.

You push yourself up. You are breathing hard. The sun finds you immediately — has been waiting, you realize, the whole time — and the white stretches in every direction except behind you, where the blue has already vanished without a trace, as though you dreamed it, as though you invented it, as though the Ocean of Unbecoming was never there at all.

You are here. You are warm. The smell of old paper lingers in your nose like a question left unanswered. The cut on your knee is bleeding steadily into the sand and it is the most real thing that has happened to you so far.

You hold on.

**What do you do?**
